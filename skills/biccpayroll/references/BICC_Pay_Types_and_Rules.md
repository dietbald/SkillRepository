# BICC Pay Types, Staff Overview & Rules

*Belgian Iloilo Construction Corp. — Updated April 2026*

---

## 1. Pay Type Definitions

### MONTHLY
Regular salaried rank-and-file employee paid a fixed semi-monthly amount. Basic pay equals the monthly rate divided by 2 per cutoff period, regardless of how many working days fall within the period. Absences, lates, leave without pay, and sick leave without pay are deducted separately.

- Basic Pay: Monthly Rate / 2 per cutoff
- Daily Rate: Monthly Rate x 12 / 313 (Mon-Sat schedule)
- Fully covered by the Labor Code — entitled to overtime pay, holiday premium, rest day premium, and general incentives
- Overtime requires prior approval — employee cannot render OT without it
- No half-day leave — employee works the whole day or takes a full day leave/absence

### DAILY
Daily-rated rank-and-file employee paid only for days actually worked. If the employee works fewer days, basic pay is proportionally lower.

- Basic Pay: Daily Rate x Days Worked
- Daily Rate: Monthly Rate x 12 / 313 (Mon-Sat schedule)
- Fully covered by the Labor Code — entitled to overtime pay, holiday premium, rest day premium, and general incentives
- Overtime requires prior approval
- "No work, no pay" applies on special non-working holidays if employee does not work
- No half-day leave — full day only

### FIXED
Employee with a fixed, all-inclusive compensation package. This category covers several types of employees who are exempt from overtime and premium pay rules under Article 82 of the Labor Code:

- **Managerial staff** — managers in the office who oversee departments or teams
- **Field personnel** — employees who regularly work away from the principal office with hours that cannot be determined with reasonable certainty
- **Site personnel and site engineers** — who manage staff and operations on construction sites
- **Sales staff** — who work primarily outside the office, measured by output/targets

Characteristics:
- Basic Pay: Monthly Rate / 2 per cutoff (same as MONTHLY)
- NOT entitled to overtime pay, holiday premium, or rest day premium
- Still subject to late/undertime deductions and absence deductions
- Leave without pay and sick leave without pay are deducted
- Still eligible for general incentives (allowances, etc.)
- No half-day leave — full day only

### EXEMPT
Employee fully exempt from attendance tracking. Paid on a monthly basis but not subject to time-in/time-out requirements and not subject to late, undertime, or absence deductions. This classification is reserved for:

- **Directors** — company directors and executives
- **Consultants** — external or internal consultants with flexible arrangements
- **Domestic helpers** — household/personal service staff

Characteristics:
- Basic Pay: Monthly Rate / 2 per cutoff
- NOT subject to attendance tracking — no late/undertime/AWOL deductions
- NOT entitled to overtime pay, holiday premium, or rest day premium
- May receive allowances or incentives at management's discretion, but typically does not have attendance-based incentives

## 2. Mandatory Deductions & Benefits Enrollment

Enrollment in SSS, HDMF (Pag-IBIG), and PhilHealth is not automatic for all employees. Some staff may be exempt from one or more of these contributions, particularly during probation or based on their employment arrangement. This applies across all four pay types.

- SSS, HDMF, and PhilHealth deductions are only applied in **Cutoff B** (never in Cutoff A)
- Employees during **probation** may not yet be enrolled in SSS/HDMF/PhilHealth
- Some employees are exempt from one or more benefits based on their arrangement
- **Withholding tax** applies to ALL employees whose taxable compensation exceeds the tax threshold — regardless of pay type or benefits enrollment status
- Tax is computed after deducting mandatory government contributions from gross pay

## 5. Detailed Scenarios

### A. Regular Working Day (Mon-Sat)

**A1. Full day (8 hours)**
MONTHLY/FIXED/EXEMPT: Regular pay (in salary). DAILY: 100% of daily rate.

**A2. Late arrival**
MONTHLY/DAILY/FIXED: Deduct late hours x hourly rate. Late minutes tracked for incentive eligibility. EXEMPT: No deduction.

**A3. Undertime (early departure)**
MONTHLY/DAILY/FIXED: Deduct undertime hours x hourly rate. Combined with late total. EXEMPT: No deduction.

**A4. Approved overtime**
MONTHLY/DAILY: OT hours x hourly rate x 125%. OT must be filed and approved BEFORE the work is performed. Employee cannot render OT without prior approval. FIXED/EXEMPT: Not entitled.

**A5. Unapproved overtime**
ALL: No OT pay. Employees cannot claim overtime without prior approval. Time beyond 8 hours is not compensated.

**A6. AWOL (absent, no leave filed)**
MONTHLY/FIXED: Deduct 1 day x daily rate. DAILY: No pay for that day. EXEMPT: No deduction.

**A7. Leave With Pay (SIL)**
MONTHLY/FIXED: No deduction, counts as worked. DAILY: Paid daily rate, counts as worked. EXEMPT: No deduction.

**A8. Leave Without Pay (LWOP)**
MONTHLY/FIXED: Deduct 1 day x daily rate. Approved but unpaid. DAILY: No pay. EXEMPT: No deduction.

**A9. Sick Leave Without Pay**
MONTHLY/FIXED: Deduct 1 day x daily rate. Same treatment as LWOP. DAILY: No pay. EXEMPT: No deduction.

**A10. Official Business Trip (OBT)**
MONTHLY/DAILY/FIXED: Counts as present. No deduction. EXEMPT: N/A (no attendance tracking).

**A11. Half-day leave request**
ALL: Not allowed. Company policy requires full-day attendance or full-day leave/absence. No half-day leave filing permitted.

### B. Sunday (Rest Day)

**B1. Employee does not work**
ALL: No pay, no deduction. Sunday is a rest day.

**B2. Employee works (approved)**
MONTHLY/DAILY: 130% of daily rate for the first 8 hours. Must be authorised. FIXED/EXEMPT: No premium — exempt.

**B3. Works + approved OT**
MONTHLY/DAILY: First 8 hrs at 130%. OT hours at hourly rate x 130% x 130% (169% effective). FIXED/EXEMPT: Not entitled.

**B4. Works without authorisation**
ALL: No premium pay. Sunday work must be pre-approved.

### C. Regular Holiday

**C1. Company closed, employee does not work**
MONTHLY/FIXED/EXEMPT: 100% — already in salary. DAILY: 100% of daily rate (paid even without work — statutory right).

**C2. Employee works**
MONTHLY/DAILY: 200% of daily rate. FIXED/EXEMPT: No premium — exempt.

**C3. Works + approved OT**
MONTHLY/DAILY: First 8 hrs at 200%. OT at hourly rate x 200% x 130% (260% effective). FIXED/EXEMPT: Not entitled.

**C4. Falls on Sunday, employee does not work**
MONTHLY/FIXED/EXEMPT: Already in salary. DAILY: 100% of daily rate.

**C5. Falls on Sunday, employee works**
MONTHLY/DAILY: 260% of daily rate (200% + 30% rest day premium on 200%). FIXED/EXEMPT: No premium.

**C6. Employee AWOL the day before the holiday**
MONTHLY/DAILY: Loses holiday pay. DOLE rule: must be present or on approved leave the day immediately before a regular holiday to be entitled. FIXED/EXEMPT: Not applicable.

**C7. Employee on approved leave the day before**
MONTHLY/DAILY: Retains holiday pay. Approved leave (LWP or LWOP) preserves entitlement. FIXED/EXEMPT: Not applicable.

### D. Special Non-Working Holiday — Company Closed

**D1. Employee does not work**
MONTHLY/FIXED/EXEMPT: No extra pay, no deduction (in salary). DAILY: No pay ("no work, no pay").

**D2. Employee is called in and works**
MONTHLY/DAILY: 130% of daily rate (+30% premium). FIXED/EXEMPT: No premium — exempt.

**D3. Works + approved OT**
MONTHLY/DAILY: First 8 hrs at 130%. OT at hourly rate x 130% x 130% (169%). FIXED/EXEMPT: Not entitled.

### E. Special Non-Working Holiday — Company Open

**E1. Employee works full day**
MONTHLY/DAILY: 130% of daily rate. The premium is triggered by the government declaration, regardless of whether the company is open or closed. FIXED/EXEMPT: No premium.

**E2. Employee does not work**
MONTHLY/FIXED/EXEMPT: Already in salary. DAILY: No pay.

**E3. Employee is late**
MONTHLY/DAILY/FIXED: Late deduction applies as normal. The 30% premium (for MONTHLY/DAILY) is based on the full day, not reduced by lates. EXEMPT: No deduction.

### F. Edge Cases

**F1. Special non-working holiday falls on Sunday, employee works**
MONTHLY/DAILY: 150% of daily rate (50% premium when rest day coincides). FIXED/EXEMPT: No premium.

**F2. Regular holiday falls on Saturday**
Saturday is a regular working day for Mon-Sat employees. Normal regular holiday rules apply (see C1-C3).

**F3. Two holidays on the same day**
MONTHLY/DAILY: The higher rate applies. Regular holiday (200%) prevails over special (130%). Not stacked. FIXED/EXEMPT: No premium.

**F4. Holiday during approved leave**
Regular holiday: Employee gets holiday pay and does NOT consume a leave credit. Special holiday: Leave credit not consumed, no extra pay.

**F5. New hire starts mid-cutoff**
MONTHLY/FIXED: Basic Pay stays at Monthly Rate / 2. Days before the start date count as AWOL → deducted as absence (AWOL × Daily Rate). Net effect is the same as proration but uses standard formulas. DAILY: Paid for days worked (automatic). EXEMPT: Prorated per arrangement.

**Allowance proration for new hires:** Use calendar days of the month: `Full Allowance × (calendar_days_worked / total_calendar_days_in_month)`. Example: Hired Mar 23, allowance 5,000/month → 5,000 × 9/31 = 1,451.61. Put prorated amount directly in column V — do NOT modify EMPLOYEE INFO.

**F6. Resignation / termination mid-cutoff**
ALL: Pay prorated to last working day. Final pay must include prorated 13th month and unused SIL conversion (if applicable).

**F7. Employee reclassified mid-period**
ALL: Split computation — old rate for days before, new rate for days after. Requires manual adjustment in the payroll.

**F8. Works on holiday, OT not approved**
MONTHLY/DAILY: Gets holiday premium for first 8 hours. Time beyond 8 hours NOT compensated (no approved OT = no OT pay, even on a holiday). FIXED/EXEMPT: Not entitled to any premium.

**F9. OT filed but fewer hours actually worked**
MONTHLY/DAILY: Pay only actual OT hours per time log. The approval is the ceiling, not a guarantee. FIXED/EXEMPT: Not applicable.

**F10. Time-in recorded but no time-out**
MONTHLY/DAILY/FIXED: Counts as present. Treat as undertime for missing hours unless corrected. Verify with supervisor. EXEMPT: N/A.

**F11. Worked on holiday without filing authorisation**
MONTHLY/DAILY: Employee still worked — premium applies by law. Flag as process violation for HR. FIXED/EXEMPT: No premium regardless.

**F12. Absent the day before a regular holiday**
MONTHLY/DAILY: Loses holiday pay (must be present or on approved leave the day before). FIXED/EXEMPT: Not applicable — no holiday pay entitlement.

**F13. Employee on probation**
Pay type rules apply the same way. However, employee may not yet be enrolled in SSS/HDMF/PhilHealth. Withholding tax still applies if compensation exceeds the threshold.

## 6. General Incentives

BICC provides various incentives to employees depending on their position and role. These are collectively referred to as "General Incentives" and are separate from base salary.

**Types of incentives may include:**
- **Attendance Incentive (col U)** — ₱1,000 in Cutoff B if employee complied with ALL three conditions for the whole month (both cutoff periods). Always 0 in Cutoff A. Conditions:
  1. Cannot be late more than 15 minutes even once (any single late >15min disqualifies)
  2. Cannot be late more than 3 times in the month (even 1 second late counts as a late instance)
  3. Cannot be absent/AWOL any day — only approved leave (LWP, LWOP, sick leave with doctor's note) is acceptable
- **1st Half Allowance** — fixed amount paid in **Cutoff A only**
- **2nd Half Allowance** — fixed amount paid in **Cutoff B only**
- **Daily Allowance** — a per-day amount counted by the number of days the employee is present during the cutoff period. This applies to any pay type if the employee has this allowance assigned, though most staff do not. Currently only Futotana, Edwyn F. has a daily allowance.
- **Position-specific allowances** — as determined by management

**V column formula:**
- Cutoff A: V = Allow_1st + Daily_Allowance × (Days_Worked - Absences)
- Cutoff B: V = Allow_2nd + Daily_Allowance × (Days_Worked - Absences)

**Eligibility:**
- MONTHLY, DAILY, and FIXED employees are eligible for general incentives
- EXEMPT employees may receive allowances or incentives at management's discretion, though they typically do not have attendance-based incentives

## 7. Period Day Counting

**Rule: Period Days = ALL Monday-to-Saturday calendar days in the period, INCLUDING holidays.**

Holidays are still calendar working days. Employees are either present, on approved leave, or absent. Holidays do NOT reduce the period day count.

Examples:
| Period | Mon-Sat Days | Holidays | Period Days |
|--------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| Mar 16-31, 2026 | 14 | Mar 18 (special), Mar 20 (regular) | **14** |
| Mar 1-15, 2026 | 12 | None | **12** |
| Feb 1-15, 2026 | 12 | Feb 11 (special) | **12** |

## 8. Attendance Data Source

**Do NOT use the ATTENDANCE SUMMARY sheet** from the external attendance file. Its formulas are unreliable — wrong day counts, error values for new hires, and incorrect late/OT aggregations.

**Instead, compute all values from the raw sheets:**

| Value | Source | Method |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Days Worked | TIME LOG + FILED OBT + FILED LEAVE | Count days with biometric IN or OBT entry + holiday credits. **For DAILY employees only:** also add LWP days, because L = Rate/Day × Days and LWP must be paid. For MONTHLY/FIXED, L = Monthly/2 regardless of days so LWP is not added to days. |
| Tardy (Late + UT) | TIME LOG + FILED OBT | Merge bio + OBT per day: effective_in = min(bio, OBT), effective_out = max(bio, OBT). Late = effective_in > 8:00 AM. Undertime = effective_out < 5:00 PM (only when biometric exists). OBT-only days: no late/UT. Special holiday: late & UT excused. |
| LWP | FILED LEAVE | Col F is a flag (not count). Count working days (Mon-Sat) in Date From → Date To range. |
| LWOP | FILED LEAVE | Col G is a flag (not count). Count working days in date range. |
| AWOL | Computed | Period Days - Days Worked - LWP - LWOP |
| OT Hours | FILED OT | Sum column J for FULLY APPROVED entries in period |
| Holiday Workers | TIME LOG | Check IN values on holiday dates |
| OBT Pairs | FILED OBT | Up to 4 IN/OUT pairs per row: cols D-E, F-G, H-I, J-K. Read ALL pairs. |

See `payroll_logic_spec.md` Section 5A for detailed computation logic.
