# DPWH RCCEM 2015 — General Provisions

Source: Road Construction Cost Estimation Manual (RCCEM) 2015, Bureau of Construction, Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH).

---

## FOREWORD

To ensure uniformity in quantity take-off and the derivation of unit cost in the estimation of DPWH projects, three (3) manuals were prepared to serve as guidelines and/or reference materials for DPWH Implementing Offices:

1. Road Construction Cost Estimation Manual
2. Bridge Construction Cost Estimation Manual
3. Building Construction Cost Estimation Manual

The objective of these cost estimation manuals is to establish unit costs using standard cost analysis for all pay items of work in the DPWH Standard Specifications for Highways, Bridges and Airports, Volume II (Blue Book).

This volume, Cost Estimation Manual for Road Construction, covers items of works in the construction, upgrading and repair/rehabilitation/reconstruction of roads and roadside structures. This will serve as guide to engineers and heads of DPWH Implementing Offices (IOs) in the preparation and review of Programs of Works (POW) and Approved Budget for the Contract (ABC).

The standard cost sheets (or Detailed Unit Price Analysis) for the items of work included herein were formulated by the Bureau of Construction in collaboration with Regional Offices and District Engineering Offices, which have adopted this 2022 version of these cost sheets with positive results. With the adoption of these updated cost sheets throughout the country, there will be consistency in the format of presentation and uniform reference for materials cost, labor and equipment rental rates. This manual also advocates that cost estimators optimize materials, local resources and equipment, complying with specifications to complete the project within budget, on time, on schedule, and at a reasonable cost.

## DEFINITION OF TERMS

### Program of Works (POW)
Is the total estimated cost of the project which covers the Civil Works Cost, Engineering Supervision and Administrative Overhead, Road Right-of-Way Acquisition and Construction Contingency, etc. The POW shall include information about the project location and limits, quantity and unit cost for each item of work, construction duration, funding source, equipment requirements, etc.

### Approved Budget for the Contract (ABC)
Is the total estimated agency estimate (direct cost, indirect cost and tax) for civil works contract of infrastructure projects. It is the ceiling of award of contract for government locally-funded projects and reference for comparison of bid costs of contractors for both locally-funded and foreign-assisted projects.

### Detailed Unit Price Analysis (DUPA)
Is the determination of reasonable unit cost of each item of work which reflects the breakdown showing the quantities of work items, unit prices of materials, equipment rental rates, labor rates and DPWH-prescribed indirect costs including taxes.

### Standard Labor Rates
Are matrices prepared and issued by Department of Public Works and Highways periodically showing the required compensation for manpower workforce of different categories, which comprises the minimum wage as approved by the NWPC, DOLE plus the monetary benefits.

### ACEL Guidebook 2014
Is a guidebook where rental rates of commonly used equipment for infrastructure projects are based. It is published by Association of Carriers and Equipment Lessors (ACEL), Inc.

### Construction Materials Price Data (CMPD)
Is a compilation of unit prices of construction materials updated quarterly per District Engineering Office to be used as reference in the pricing of materials component of the pay items of work in the preparation of DUPA, ABC & POW, including new items of work involved in a VO.

### DPWH Standard Specifications for Public Works and Highways
Is a compilation of detailed descriptions of work items in constructing public works and highways projects such as roads, bridges, buildings, etc. In other words, a compilation of specifications of standards works acceptable to the DPWH.

## INTRODUCTION

This manual covers the establishment of standard cost sheets for each pay item of work for Road Construction with established quantity of materials component, manpower and equipment requirement based on the DPWH Standard Specifications for Highways, Bridges and Airports. This includes setting of travel speed in hauling construction materials for each type of road surface condition and formula in calculating the total cycle time.

### Objectives

1. To provide a uniform reference/basis by the DPWH Implementing Offices in arriving at the unit cost for each pay item in the preparation of the Program of Works (POW) and Approved Budget for the Contract (ABC) in the implementation of various DPWH infrastructure projects.
2. To minimize cost leakage of the project in line with the Department's policy in the implementation of infrastructure projects to be a Right Project, at Right Cost and Right Quality.

### The following Direct Cost components are standardized:

- **Materials Component** — established standard quantity of component materials for each pay item based on its specifications taking into consideration factors for wastage, shrinkage, swell and bulking.
- **Labor Component** — established the appropriate labor category, number and production output of manpower resources required for each pay item of work.
- **Equipment Component** — established the appropriate type, capacity and production output of equipment resources required for each item of work.

### Price Data References

- **Materials Price** — based on the price data of construction materials established by the DPWH District Engineering Office and submitted to the DPWH Central Office Price Monitoring Committee in accordance with D.O. No. 71, Series of 2012.
- **Labor Rates** — based on the labor rates established for each District Engineering Office, comprising the minimum wage plus the allowances applicable in the locality which includes benefits calculated in accordance with D.O. No. 71, Series of 2012.
- **Equipment Rates** — based from ACEL Equipment Guidebook of 2014, Edition 25, per Department Order No. 22, Series of 2015, duly signed by Secretary Rogelio L. Singson and D.O. No. 22, Series of 2015. For equipment not available in ACEL, rental rates shall be taken from the rates prepared by the Bureau of Equipment.

## GENERAL NOTES

1. The capacity of equipment as presented in the standard cost sheet for each item of work may be adjusted to a higher or lower capacity, provided the resulting unit cost is more economical. Rental rates shall be based on ACEL Guidebook of 2014.

   **Example:** In hauling of materials, use of Dump Truck with higher capacity of fifteen (15) m³ instead of ten (10) m³ capacity as presented will result to a lower price, as the capacity gets bigger, the lower the resulting cost at PHP 45.46/m³ of hauling cost from PHP 54.15/m³. Furthermore, the utilization of a Payloader will be optimized.

2. The capacity of 10 (ten) m³ for the Dump Truck as presented may be reduced in cases of extraordinary conditions where the road has a very steep gradient, in a severely deteriorated condition or where the project involves a narrow road not accessible during the actual hauling to load the full capacity of the Dump Truck. A proper substantiation/justifications shall be submitted however in the preparation of estimates.

3. For items of work that involves hauling and disposal of materials where hauling distance is indicated, in cases when actual hauling distance is shorter or longer than the assumed hauling distance of 3 km in the standard cost sheets, the number of equipment hours for Dump Truck shall be adjusted based on the cycle time analysis. The actual hauling distance should be the nearest point approved and determined by the Implementing Office prior to the preparation of the ABC/POW and shall be indicated in the DUPA.

4. For equipment utilization of less than one (1) day, the scheduled operating hours shall be paid at operation per hour and the remaining hours of the minimum 8 hours a day shall be considered for payment as bare rental rates.

5. The utilization of equipment other than the equipment model and capacity presented in the standard cost sheets may be allowed, provided there is a corresponding adjustment in the substitution. Rental rates of equipment should be based from ACEL Guidebook or the Bureau of Equipment Rental Rates guidelines.

6. The presented DUPA is based on a one (1) gang of labor and equipment with the specified output per day. A corresponding adjustment to additional equipment and labor may be made in the substitution.

7. The application of Mark-Up (OCM and Profit) shall be in accordance with the ranges of the Total Estimated Direct Cost (EDC) of the project as prescribed under D.O. No. 22, Series of 2015.

## SECTION A: COST ESTIMATION PROCESS

### Flowchart Summary

The cost estimation process flows through the following offices:

```
DEO ──────────────── Consultant
 │
 ▼ Is project within limits of authority delegated to DEO?
Yes → (approve at DEO level)
No  → RO ──── UPMO
              │
              ▼ Is project within limits of authority delegated to RO?
             Yes → (approve at RO level)
             No  → BOD & BOC → Assistant Secretary / Undersecretary / Secretary
```

### District Engineering Office (DEO)

1. The Estimator shall determine the items of work involved based on the Approved Plans.
2. The Estimator shall prepare the DUPA, ABC & POW including its attachments applying the items of work involved based on the latest standard labor rates & CMPD, standard cost sheets and D.O. No. 22, Series of 2015.
3. The District Engineer shall approve the ABC & POW, if the project is within the limits of authority delegated to him/her.
4. If beyond the authority of the DEO, forward the DUPA, ABC & POW and Approved Construction Plans together with the required attachments to the Regional Office for review/evaluation and approval, if the project is within the limits of the delegated authority of the Regional Director.
5. If Planning and Design Division of the Regional Office will make an adjustment in the quantities and delete/add pay item/s in the submitted construction plans, the ABC & POW shall be adjusted accordingly by the District Engineering Office and resubmitted the same for approval.

### Regional Office (RO)

1. The Estimator shall determine the items of work involved based on the Approved Plans.
2. The Estimator shall prepare the DUPA, ABC & POW including its attachments applying the items of work involved based on the latest standard labor rates & CMPD, standard cost sheets and D.O. No. 22, Series of 2015.
3. The Regional Director shall approve the ABC & POW, if the project is within the limits of authority delegated to him/her.
4. If beyond the authority of the RO, forward the DUPA, ABC & POW and Approved Construction Plans together with the required attachments to the Bureau of Construction (BOC) and Bureau of Design (BOD) respectively, for review/evaluation for approval of the Assistant Secretary for Technical Services, Undersecretary for Technical Services or Secretary depending on the limits of authority.
5. If Bureau of Design shall make an adjustment in the quantities and delete/add pay item/s in the submitted construction plans, the ABC & POW shall be adjusted accordingly by the Regional Office and resubmitted the same for approval.

### Unified Project Management Office (UPMO)

1. The Consultant shall determine the items of work involved based on the Approved Plans.
2. The Consultant shall prepare the DUPA, ABC & POW including its attachments applying the items of work involved.
3. The Project Director shall conduct review/evaluation of the proposed ABC & POW and recommends its approval to the authorized approving official concerned.
4. Forward the ABC & POW and Approved Construction Plans together with the required attachments to the Bureau of Construction (BOC) and Bureau of Design (BOD) respectively, for review/evaluation to be approved by the Assistant Secretary for Technical Services, Undersecretary for Technical Services or Secretary depending on the limits of authority.
5. If Bureau of Design shall make an adjustment in the quantities and delete/add pay item/s in the submitted construction plans, the ABC & POW shall be adjusted accordingly by the concerned UPMO and resubmitted the same for approval.

### Approval Thresholds — Legal Bases

Approval of the ABC & POW shall be based on the following Department Orders:

1. **Department Order No. 91, Series of 2012** — Authority of the Assistant Secretary for Technical Services to Approve Plans, Program of Works and Approved Budget for the Contract for DPWH Locally-Funded and Foreign-Assisted Civil Works Projects Costing more than PhP50 Million up to PhP100 Million.

2. **Department Order No. 95, Series of 2013** — Reissuance of D.O. No. 9, S. 2011 Re: Conduct of Pre-Procurement, Procurement, and Implementation Activities for DPWH Foreign-Assisted Civil Works Projects.

3. **Department Order No. 109, Series of 2014** — Approval of Plans, Program of Works (POW) and Approved Budget for the Contract for DPWH Locally-Funded Projects Costing PhP50 Million and below implemented by Unified Project Management Offices.

4. **Department Order No. 140, Series of 2014** — Increased Limits of Delegated Authority to Regional Directors to Approve Plans, Program of Works (POW) and Approved Budget for the Contract (ABC) of Locally-Funded Projects.

5. **Department Order No. 54, Series of 2011** — Revised Limits of Responsibilities and Authorities of Concerned DPWH Offices and Officials in the Conduct of Pre-Procurement, Procurement, and Implementation Activities for DPWH Locally-Funded Civil Works Projects.

6. **Department Order No. 5, Series of 2015** — Revised Limits of Delegated Authority to Approve Program of Works (POW) and Approved Budget for the Contract (ABC) and Procure Projects.

## SECTION B: STANDARD DETAILED UNIT PRICE ANALYSIS (DUPA) SHEETS

### What the DUPA Sheet Covers

Section B contains the Standard DUPA Sheets for all Road Construction pay items. These are organized into:

- **Part C** — Earthworks (pages 14 onward)
- **Part D** — Subbase and Base Course (page 65 onward)
- **Part E** — Surface Course (page 74 onward)
- **Part F** — Drainage and Slope Protection Structures (page 131 onward)
- **Part H** — Miscellaneous Structures (page 186 onward)

Additional reference sections:

- **Section C** — Calculation of Cycle Time for Hauling Equipment (page 242)
- **Section D** — Computation of Labor Rates (page 243)
- **Section E** — Equipment Rental Rates (page 246)

### DUPA Sheet Format — Structure

Each DUPA sheet follows this standard layout:

**Header Information:**
- Item No. / Description
- Unit of Measurement
- Output per hour / per day

**Part A — Labor**

| Designation | No. of Persons | No. of Hours | Hourly Rate | Amount (PhP) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Construction Foreman | (qty) | (hrs) | (rate) | (amount) |
| Skilled Labor / Unskilled Labor | (qty) | (hrs) | (rate) | (amount) |

- Sub-Total for A = sum of all labor amounts
- **Note:** Labor rates are variable per Region/District based on minimum wage as approved by DOLE. Refer to the Schedule of Labor Rates.

**Part B — Equipment**

| Name and Capacity | No. of Units | No. of Hours | Hourly Rate | Amount (PhP) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| (Equipment type and capacity) | (qty) | (hrs) | (rate) | (amount) |

- Sub-Total for B = sum of all equipment amounts
- Type (A + B) = Sub-Total A + Sub-Total B (direct cost before materials)
- **Note:** For hauling items, the Dump Truck hours are adjusted based on the actual hauling cycle time computed per Section C.

**Part C — Materials**

| Name and Specification | Unit | Quantity | Unit Cost | Amount (PhP) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| (Material description) | (unit) | (qty) | (unit cost) | (amount) |

- Sub-Total for F (Materials subtotal)
- **Note:** Material prices are variable per Region/District based on CMPD. Refer to the Construction Materials Price Data.

**Subtotal Calculation Formula:**

```
Direct Unit Cost (C) = Sub-Total A + Sub-Total B + Sub-Total F
                       (Labor)    + (Equipment)  + (Materials)

OCM (Overhead, Contingencies & Miscellaneous) = OCM% × C
Contractor's Profit (P)                        = Profit% × C
Value Added Tax (VAT)                          = 5% × (C + OCM + P)

Total Unit Cost = C + OCM + P + VAT
```

**OCM and Profit Percentage Ranges** (per D.O. No. 22, Series of 2015):

The OCM% and Profit% applied shall be in accordance with the ranges prescribed relative to the Total Estimated Direct Cost (EDC) of the project. These ranges are prescribed in D.O. No. 22, Series of 2015 and shall be applied consistently across all DUPA items for a given project.

- **VAT** is calculated as 5% of the sum of Direct Cost + OCM + Profit.

**Footer Notes (standard on each DUPA sheet):**
- "The presented DUPA is based on a one (1) gang of labor and equipment with the specified output of [X] hrs. To increase the output, additional equipment and labor may be added."
- "Part C - Earthworks" (or applicable Part designation)
