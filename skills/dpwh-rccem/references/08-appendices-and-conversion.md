## APPENDIX D — COMPUTATION OF LABOR RATES

---

## D.1 BASIS OF COMPUTATION

The estimated labor rates for each region/district office were computed in accordance with **D.O. No. 40, Series of 2009**: "Re: Guidelines for the establishment of Construction Materials Price Data, Standard Labor and Equipment Rental Rates," and based on the minimum wage rate as published by the National Wages and Productivity Commission (NWPC), Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE).

The computed labor rates **include all component costs** such as:
- Basic wage
- Cost of living allowance (COLA)
- Workers' statutory monetary benefits
- Other allowances

These are computed in accordance with existing laws and government regulations. The rates **shall be updated** as the need arises based on new minimum wages set by the NWPC/DOLE and/or any amendments to the benefits of the workers.

---

## D.2 CONDITIONS IN THE USAGE OF THE LABOR RATES

**CASE I:** In cases where the project/s traverses certain barangays, municipalities, towns or cities (with varying labor rates) of a concerned DEO, the **average labor cost of the said places shall be utilized**.

**CASE II:** In cases wherein the project/s traverses certain provinces within the jurisdiction of only one (1) Regional Office, the **average labor cost of the said Regional Office shall be utilized**.

**CASE III:** In cases wherein project/s passes numerous provinces within the jurisdiction of two (2) or more Regional Offices, the **average labor cost of the said Regional Office with the longest work coverage or with largest major scope of affected localities shall be utilized**.

## D.4 NOTES ON COMPUTATION COMPONENTS

The NCR rate components (per the sample tabulation) are computed as follows:

| Component | Basis |
|---|---|
| Minimum Wage Rate | = PHP 466.00 (NCR, 2015) |
| Basic Daily Compensation | = Minimum Wage (multiplied by Labor Cost Factor / Grade of work category) |
| Leave (Sick/Vacation) | = Basic Daily Wage × 13/260 days per year |
| Bonus (13th Month Pay) | = Basic Monthly Wage / 12 |
| SSS | = Based on SSS Monthly Contribution (Employee + Employer) |
| PHIC (PhilHealth) | = 1% of Monthly Compensation before rounding (Employer share) |
| PAG-IBIG | = 2% of Monthly Compensation below PHP 5,000.00; above PHP 5,000.00 = PHP 100/month |
| Employee's Compensation Contribution | = PHP 10.00 per month |
| Daily Rate | = Monthly Rate / 26 days per month |
| Hourly Rate | = Daily Rate / 8 hrs per day |

## APPENDIX E — EQUIPMENT RENTAL RATES

---

## E.1 BASIS OF COMPUTATION

Rental of equipment presented in the Detailed Unit Price Analysis (DUPA) are based on the prevailing **2014 ACE Rates** (Association of Carriers and Equipment Lessors, ACEL, Inc.), i.e., rental rates approved for use by the DPWH.

For equipment not indicated in the ACE booklet, rates shall be **taken from the rental rates prepared by the Bureau of Equipment**.

For simplicity in computation, the operated rates are preferred over the bare rental rates, as the former includes operator's wages, fringe benefits, fuel, oil, lubricants and equipment maintenance.

## E.2 GENERAL NOTES ON EQUIPMENT RENTAL RATES

1. Rental rates presented are based on **prevailing 2014 ACEL rates** for equipment approved for use by DPWH.
2. For equipment **not listed in the ACE booklet**, rates shall be taken from rental rates prepared by the Bureau of Equipment.
3. For simplicity, **operated rates** are preferred over bare rental rates, as they include:
   - Operator wages
   - Fringe benefits
   - Fuel, oil, and lubricants
   - Equipment maintenance
4. Minor Tools are computed as a **percentage of the direct labor cost** (typically 5%–15% depending on the work item), not rented equipment — these are small tools (picks, shovels, wheelbarrows, etc.) expensed under the equipment portion.
5. All rates are in **Philippine Pesos (PHP)** per hour unless otherwise stated.
6. Rates are **exclusive of mobilization/demobilization** unless specifically included in the bid item.

---

## E.3 MINOR TOOLS COMPUTATION

In the DUPA sheets, "Minor Tools" is listed under Part B — Equipment as:
- **5%–15% of Direct Labor Cost (Part A sub-total)**

This covers: hand tools, shovels, pickaxes, wheelbarrows, levels, hammers, measuring equipment, safety equipment, etc.

Typical rates used per item type:

| Work Type | Minor Tools Rate |
|---|---|
| Earthwork items | 5% of Labor Cost |
| Concrete / masonry | 5–10% of Labor Cost |
| Slope protection / riprap | 5–15% of Labor Cost |
| Pipe installation | 10% of Labor Cost |
| Painting / marking | 10% of Labor Cost |
| Tree planting / sodding | 10% of Labor Cost |

## E.7 DIRECT COST FORMULA

```
Direct Unit Cost = A + B + C
```
Where:
- **A** = Labor Cost (sum of Designation × No. of Persons × No. of Hours × Hourly Rate)
- **B** = Equipment Cost (sum of Name × No. of Units × No. of Hours × Hourly Rate)
- **C** = Materials Cost (sum of Material × Quantity × Unit Cost)

```
Overhead, Contingencies & Miscellaneous (OCM) = 12% of Direct Cost
Contractor's Profit (CP) = 8% of Direct Cost
Value Added Tax (VAT) = 5% of (Direct Cost + OCM + Profit)

Total Unit Cost = Direct Cost + OCM + Profit + VAT
```

Or in formula form:
```
Total Unit Cost = DC × (1 + 0.12 + 0.08) × 1.05
               = DC × 1.20 × 1.05
               = DC × 1.26
```

---

## E.8 NOTES ON DPWH STANDARD COST FORMULA

- **OCM (12%)** covers: Overhead expenses, contingencies, and miscellaneous charges of the contractor.
- **Profit (8%)** is the contractor's profit margin allowed under DPWH guidelines.
- **VAT (5%)** is applied to the sum of Direct Cost + OCM + Profit (i.e., the pre-VAT total).
- The above percentages are the **standard DPWH RCCEM 2015 rates**. They may be adjusted by special order or regional memorandum.
- The **Total Unit Cost** from the DUPA is the basis for the **unit price** entered in the Bill of Quantities (BOQ).
