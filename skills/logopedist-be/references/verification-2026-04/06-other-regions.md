## Item 1 — DG-specific decree numbers for inclusion / Förderpädagogik

**Status:** PARTIAL (foundational decree confirmed; no successor "inclusion" decree replacing it; ongoing reform agenda for late-2024/2025/2026 captured)

### Findings

- The **foundational text** for the German-speaking Community's special-needs and integrative-education system is the **Dekret vom 11. Mai 2009** *über das Zentrum für Förderpädagogik, zur Verbesserung der sonderpädagogischen Förderung in den Regel- und Förderschulen sowie zur Unterstützung der Förderung von Schülern mit Beeinträchtigung, Anpassungs- oder Lernschwierigkeiten in den Regel- und Förderschulen* (M.B. / Belgisches Staatsblatt, 2009). It established the **Zentrum für Förderpädagogik (ZFP)** in Eupen as a *Dienst mit getrennter Geschäftsführung* effective 1 September 2009.
- Underlying philosophy ("Jeder Schüler ist ein Förderschüler"): every school is an adapted school, every pupil is a pupil with particular needs. This decree organises three blocks: (a) *Förderschulen* run by the ZFP, (b) *Integrationsprojekte* in mainstream schools, (c) the *Kompetenzzentrum* providing in-school advisory support for any school in the DG. There is **no separate "inclusion decree"** that replaced the 2009 text — instead, the DG has updated provisions through the annual *Dekret über Maßnahmen im Unterrichtswesen* (omnibus education decrees) and through reorganisation of the ZFP.
- The DG's foundational education law (the **Dekret vom 31. August 1998** über den Auftrag an die Schulträger und das Schulpersonal sowie über die allgemeinen pädagogischen und organisatorischen Bestimmungen) remains in force; it still anchors the school-system framework into which Förderpädagogik plugs.
- **Recent omnibus decrees** modifying education (and Förderpädagogik provisions) confirmed:
  - **Dekret über Maßnahmen im Unterrichtswesen 2023** — adopted by the PDG in third reading on 17 May 2023 (announced on Ostbelgien Live and BRF). Modified provisions on staff, school organisation and pedagogy, including aspects of integration support.
  - **Dekret über Maßnahmen im Unterrichtswesen 2024** — first reading by the DG government on 5 October 2023, third (final) reading on 28 March 2024. Total of 183 articles touching personnel, school organisation, partial abolition of homework in primary, kindergarten-assistant supervision powers, etc. **No standalone replacement of the 2009 Förderpädagogik decree.**
- **Ongoing reform of the ZFP itself:** since 2021, the DG government has been preparing to convert the ZFP from a *Dienst mit getrennter Geschäftsführung* of the Ministry into an **independent, network-neutral public-law institution** (*netzunabhängige Einrichtung öffentlichen Rechts*), absorbing the ZFP plus the *Pater-Damian-Fördergrundschule* of the FSU network. A *Gemeinsame Absichtserklärung* was signed on 21 December 2021. The new institution was originally planned to start operating in **2024**; the Eurydice page (last updated 25 September 2024) and ZFP's own historical pages still describe the 2009-vintage structure, suggesting the reform has either slipped or completed silently. **Re-verify this single point before publishing customer-facing material.**
- **Logopädie in the decree:** the 2009 decree explicitly enables the ZFP to deliver multidisciplinary support inside both Förderschulen and Regelschulen (via the Kompetenzzentrum). Logopädie staff (alongside Ergotherapie, Kinesitherapie, Pädagogik) work at Kaleido and inside ZFP integration teams. The decree text itself does not enumerate professional silos; concrete logopedic provisions are delivered via Kaleido (for screening and advice) and via ZFP integration teams (for in-school support). There is **no separate DG decree on logopedic support in schools** — the federal RIZIV nomenclature remains the funding chain for curative individual therapy regardless of school setting.

## Item 2 — Auxiliaires logopédiques in centres PMS

**Status:** RESOLVED

### Findings

- The **auxiliaire logopédique** function in centres PMS was created by the **reform of the centres PMS adopted by the FWB Parliament on 2 May 2019** (*décret modifiant diverses dispositions relatives aux centres psycho-médico-sociaux*). The new positions were operational as of **1 June 2019** and were funded under the *soutien à la réussite scolaire en maternel* envelope of the Pacte pour un Enseignement d'excellence.
- **Mission of the auxiliaire logopédique:** observation, identification and *prévention* of learning difficulties in maternel/primaire — explicitly *not* curative therapy. They work inside PMS teams that are linked to specific schools.
- **Recruitment profile:** restricted to holders of a 3-year *bachelier en logopédie*. The reform did **not** open the function to holders of a 5-year *master en logopédie*, which is the structural cause of the UPLF dispute.
- **UPLF position (still standing in 2026):** the Union Professionnelle des Logopèdes Francophones characterised the reform as "discriminatory" against master-level logopèdes and continues to plead for a **single university master's training pathway** aligned with European recommendations. UPLF acknowledged the underlying need for early-childhood detection but objected to the auxiliary status and to the limitation to bachelor-level recruitment. The cabinet of the FWB education minister at the time argued that bachelor-level was a *minimum* and that masters could in principle apply, but the salary scale remained pegged to the bachelor level — UPLF disputed this reading.
- **Current status (2025/2026):** the function is **still in force**. Multiple PMS centres (libre and officiel networks) are actively publishing 2025/2026 recruitment notices for *auxiliaire logopédique* positions — examples confirmed during this verification:
  - Ville de Liège — recruitment for a *centre PMS 2 — Liège* posted at https://www.liege.be/fr/vivre-a-liege/enseignement/enseignement-communal-liegeois/carriere/la-ville-de-liege-recrute-un-auxiliaire-logopedique-h-f-x-centre-pms-2
  - Centre PMS libre Wavre 1 (18 h/week, half-time, from 24 November 2025)
  - Centre PMS libre Jodoigne (7 h/week, 1/5 time, from 1 September 2025)
  - UPLF *job board* listing replacement *auxiliaire logopédique* posts for the Charleroi area: https://www.uplf.be/job/remplacement-auxiliaire-logopedique-region-de-charleroi/
- **No litigation or repeal:** no published court decision overturned the 2019 reform; no successor decree restricted or abolished the function. The status quo persists.

## Item 3 — Pôles territoriaux precise current count

**Status:** RESOLVED

### Findings

- The current count of **48 pôles territoriaux** across the **10 education zones** of the Fédération Wallonie-Bruxelles is **still accurate as of the 2024–2025 and 2025–2026 school years**. This figure is repeated verbatim by:
  - the *Pacte pour un Enseignement d'excellence* portal,
  - handicap.brussels (which describes the FWB pôles for Brussels-resident families),
  - the Ligue de l'enseignement,
  - CSC Enseignement, and
  - apeda.be parent-information pages.
- The **liste des pôles territoriaux** that enseignement.be publishes (PDF) was last *publicly* dated **20 December 2022** in its first stable form. Subsequent updates have been integrated through ministerial circulars, notably:
  - **Circulaire 8985 du 14/07/2023** — *Pôles territoriaux — circulaire de rentrée 2023–2024* (CECP-hosted PDF available).
  - **Circulaire 9202 du 19/01/2024** — WBE network — *Appel aux candidats Pôles territoriaux* (designation of staff members).
  - **Circulaire 9357** — operational rules for the 2024–2025 school year, including the framework for *aménagements raisonnables*.
  - **Circulaire 9466** of March 2025 — WBE network — *Appel aux candidats Pôles territoriaux Désignation* (next round of staff designations).
- **No published ministerial decision created or suppressed a pôle territorial in 2024–2026** that would change the headline figure of 48. Internal staff designations and convention renewals have been adjusted via the circulars above, but the *number* and *zonal distribution* remain stable.
- **Composition unchanged:** each pôle is a multidisciplinary team of at least 15 staff (teachers, educators, *logopèdes*, kinés, ergos, psychologues), attached to an *école siège* in specialised education and contracted with mainstream schools through *conventions de coopération*.
- **For SaaS integration:** treat 48 pôles / 10 zones as the stable 2025/2026 figure. Re-pull the official PDF list from `enseignement.be` (page 23827) once a year, and watch for circulars on the WBE / SeGEC / CPEONS portals at the start of each school year for staffing changes.

## Item 4 — Post-2024 residual list of material aids (PHARE / AVIQ / DSL vs mutualités/CAAMI)

**Status:** RESOLVED

### Findings

**Effective date and scope.** Since **1 January 2024**, the bulk of *aides matérielles individuelles pour personnes en situation de handicap* in Brussels (and broadly in the parallel AVIQ / DSL tracks) have been transferred to the **federal mutualité / CAAMI track**. The transfer is operational on the COCOF/PHARE side, the COCOM/Iriscare side handles the residual nomenclature in Brussels, and the file flow now starts at the patient's mutuality.

**Residual PHARE list (COCOF — French-speaking Brussels).** Service PHARE retains direct competence for a strictly limited list:

1. *Interventions pour l'accompagnement pédagogique* dans la formation professionnelle ou l'enseignement supérieur.
2. *Transcriptions de cours* dans l'enseignement obligatoire, la formation professionnelle ou l'enseignement supérieur.
3. *Vidéo-loupes avec fonction double caméra* utilisées exclusivement dans le cadre scolaire.
4. *Aides individuelles accordées exclusivement dans le cadre de l'adaptation du poste de travail ou de formation* (workplace / training-post adaptation).

**Categories transferred to mutualités / CAAMI / Iriscare nomenclature** (Brussels) — explicitly include all general material aids: **medical beds, Braille devices, home adaptations, communication aids, mobility aids, hearing-related equipment** (where not covered as ORL implants), absorbent products, etc. Communication devices and AAC tablets/software fall under the mutualité track by default; if a device is *not in the Iriscare nomenclature*, the file route is to request a *dérogation* from Iriscare via the patient's mutuality, **not** PHARE.

**PHARE website closure.** The Service PHARE website (`phare.irisnet.be`) closed permanently on **4 November 2025** because it no longer met technological and accessibility standards. The content has migrated to **`ccf.brussels`** (the COCOF Public Service portal) and to **`handicap.brussels`** (the Brussels disability information portal). For 2026, the canonical citation order should be:

1. `ccf.brussels/nos-services/personnes-handicapees/service-phare/...` for PHARE forms and rules.
2. `handicap.brussels/...` for cross-cutting Brussels disability information (including the integrated *aides à l'autonomie* explanations).
3. `iriscare.brussels` for the COCOM nomenclature and benefit-payment side.

**Walloon (AVIQ) parallel.** The AVIQ side runs the same logic — the bulk of individual material aids transferred to the mutualité track on 1 January 2024, with AVIQ retaining residual competence for school/training/work integration aids. AVIQ also published in 2025 a separate update on **mobility aids** (*aides à la mobilité*) — these now follow the federal *bandagistes* nomenclature route via INAMI/RIZIV; AVIQ has reduced its parallel intervention to specific cases.

**DG / DSL parallel.** Same federalisation pattern; DSL retains residual competence for non-INAMI-covered devices and for school/training aids in the DG, with the federal mutualité serving as primary entry point. No DG-specific exception identified for logopedic AAC equipment.

**SaaS implication.** A feature that lets a logopedist generate or pre-fill an aide-matérielle request must:
- default to the **mutualité track** for any communication device, AAC tablet, hearing aid, mobility aid, home adaptation, etc.;
- only fall back on PHARE / AVIQ / DSL when the request matches the residual categories above (workplace/training adaptation, course transcription, pedagogical accompaniment, school-only video-magnifier);
- treat the cut-off date as **strict 1 January 2024**: any unfinished pre-2024 file had to be invoiced to PHARE within 6 months (general) or within 2 years (home adaptations); after that the patient must reapply through the mutuality.

## Item 5 — BAP envelope current figure (2025/2026)

**Status:** PARTIAL

### Findings

- The **per-beneficiary annual amounts** remain in the published range of **roughly €5,000 to €35,000**, with a **flat rate of €18,653.21 per year** for full-time day-and-night services (indexed at 1 January 2023). The wikiwiph and AVIQ canonical pages have not yet been updated with newer indexation as of the verification window.
- **The total annual envelope** for BAP in Wallonia is **not directly published as a single round figure on AVIQ's public pages**. The figure cited in file 06 (~€4.4M for 2024) is consistent with sector reports but is not contradicted or updated by AVIQ's own portal in early 2026.
- **Walloon Budget 2026 explicitly touches BAP.** The 2026 regional budget — adopted on the basis of the *Accord du Gouvernement wallon sur le budget 2026* announced 20 October 2025 — includes:
  - A **decrease of €1.5 million on the BAP line**, justified by a structural under-consumption of approximately 30% in recent years (the BAP envelope had been chronically under-spent because the eligibility procedure is restrictive and waiting lists are long).
  - An **increase of €5 million for "situations prioritaires"** (urgent disability cases), which is a separate AVIQ-Handicap line and not the BAP envelope per se, but it is the closest functional substitute for the BAP for high-need cases.
- **AVIQ-wide budget context:** the 2026 Walloon Budget imposes structural savings of **€270.4 million** across the region. AVIQ specifically absorbs **€28.3 million in mission refocusing** plus **€5.6 million in operating-allocation freeze** (the dotation de gestion is not indexed in 2026). On top of that, the agency took **€10 million of facultative-subsidy cuts** in the 2025 exercise (with health and disability core funding "protected"). The sector press characterised these cuts as "doing more with less" rather than as a direct reduction of statutory benefits.
- **Family-allowance change (collateral, not BAP):** the 2026 budget eliminates duplicate *allocation familiale supplémentaire* (AFS) for children with disabilities when a federal disability allowance is already paid (≈€400,000 savings line). This affects the AVIQ-Familles branch, not BAP.

**Conclusion on item 5.** The original ~€4.4M figure for 2024 is **not invalidated** by current sources, but no AVIQ press release confirms an updated 2025 figure either. For 2026, the precise BAP line drops by €1.5M (so the 2026 envelope is on the order of **€3M plus residual indexation**, depending on the 2024/2025 baseline). For SaaS-facing copy, recommend describing the BAP envelope as "in the low single-digit millions of euros, with a structural -€1.5M cut in the 2026 Walloon budget and a parallel +€5M reinforcement of the 'situations prioritaires' line that addresses similar needs". Re-verify when AVIQ publishes its 2025 *rapport annuel* and the next *Manuel des subventions*.
