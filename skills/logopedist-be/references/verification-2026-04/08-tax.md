## Item 1 — PC 330 vs PC 200 for a private logopedie praktijk employer — PARTIAL (clear conclusion)

**Conclusion**: A pure ambulant private logopedie praktijk that employs another logopedist on an employment contract falls under **PC 330.04** ("Gezondheidsinrichtingen en -diensten — overige"), NOT the residual PC 200.

**Evidence**:

The official scope of PC 330.04 as reproduced by ACLVB, Salar, CGSLB, Acerta and others (derived from the royal decree establishing the sub-committee) is:

> "de eerste-hulpdiensten, de ondernemingen uit de bedrijfstak van het onafhankelijk ziekenvervoer, **de kabinetten van huisartsen, specialisten, kinesitherapeuten, tandartsen en andere paramedici**, de medisch-pediatrische centra, de overlegplatforms van psychiatrische instellingen en diensten, de polyklinieken, de equipes voor palliatieve thuiszorg, de externe diensten voor preventie en bescherming op het werk, de laboratoria (bijv. voor klinische biologie, pathologische anatomie, …), medische controlediensten"

Logopedists are a recognised paramedical profession under the KB of 20 October 1994, so their private cabinets fall squarely under "**andere paramedici**" in this list.

Secondary evidence:

- ACLVB lists logopedisten as being covered by PC 330 wage scales (IFIC levels 14/15/16 depending on role).
- CP 330 PDF wage scales (SOM, som.be) explicitly include logopedist function codes.
- Securex lex4you and Salar describe PC 330.04 as the "kabinetten van huisartsen / specialisten / kinesitherapeuten / tandartsen" subsector.
- PC 200 is the residual auxiliary committee; its application is strictly subsidiary (only when no sectoral PC applies). Because PC 330.04 explicitly covers "andere paramedici", PC 200 is NOT competent for an ambulant paramedical cabinet.

**Edge cases and nuances**:

- **Multi-disciplinary praktijk**: if the dominant activity (KBO/BCE NACEBEL code) is a paramedical profession (logopedie, kinesitherapie, etc.), PC 330.04 applies. If the dominant activity is commercial (e.g. a bookshop that also hires a logopedist), the auxiliary or sector PC of the dominant activity applies.
- **BV owning its own building**: the BV's paritair comité follows its main operational activity, not the real-estate holding. A praktijk BV whose NACEBEL code is 86.906 "paramedische praktijken" remains in PC 330.04 even if it also holds the building.
- **Freelancer-contracting model**: freelancers are self-employed, so no PC applies to them at all. Paritair comité rules only bind employees.
- **Employer with 0 employees**: no PC is attributed — PCs only become relevant once an employment contract exists.
- **Flexi-job extension**: since 1 January 2023, flexi-jobs are authorised in PC 330 (healthcare sector), which reinforces the reach of PC 330.04 for private practices hiring part-time help.

**Historical debate resolved**: the residual discussion (file 08 previously noted "historical debate whether PC 330 applies or PC 200 is competent") is effectively closed since the wording of PC 330.04 explicitly includes "andere paramedici". The older uncertainty dated from the pre-2006 period when the sub-committees had not yet been fleshed out in the current form. **The correct per-employer verification path** remains: check the KBO/BCE activity code and confirm with the social secretariat (Acerta, Securex, SD Worx, Liantis, Partena), but the default answer for an ambulant private logopedie cabinet is PC 330.04.

**PARTIAL note**: I did not locate a published FOD WASO ruling or ministerial circular that specifically mentions logopedisten by name — only the generic "andere paramedici" wording. An authoritative individual ruling from the Commissie Arbeidsrelaties would be the gold standard but is not required given the scope text is unambiguous.

## Item 3 — VVPRbis rate rise 15% → 18% programmawet timing — RESOLVED

**Conclusion**: As of 2026-04-06 the programmawet raising VVPRbis from 15% to 18% has **NOT yet been published** in the Belgian Staatsblad. The vote was scheduled for 26 March 2026 in the Chamber plenary but was postponed to after 1 April 2026 (pending NAR advice). The rise will enter into force on the first day of the month after publication — the earliest possible date is **1 May 2026**.

**Key facts**:

- Draft law reference: **Wetsontwerp programmawet, DOC 56-1378/001**, Kamer 2025-2026, deposited 23 February 2026.
- Budget agreement: De Wever government (24 November 2025) announced the rise.
- First programmawet of 2025 (wet van 18 juli 2025, Staatsblad 29 July 2025) did NOT contain the VVPRbis rise — only unrelated fiscal measures.
- The current draft combines VVPRbis rise with liquidation-reserve changes.
- Entry-into-force rule: **eerste dag van de maand volgend op de maand van publicatie** in het Staatsblad.
- Earliest realistic effective date: **1 May 2026**.
- Vote as of 2026-04-06: still postponed; no publication yet.

**Transition rules (as currently drafted)**:

- **VVPRbis dividends**: NO transition regime planned. All dividend distributions paid from the effective date will be taxed at 18%, regardless of when the underlying reserves were built up. Small companies can still distribute dividends at 15% roerende voorheffing up to and including 30 April 2026 (assuming 1 May 2026 entry-into-force).
- **Liquidation reserves**: distinction based on build-up date:
  - Reserves constituted through 30 December 2025 retain the old regime (6.5% after a 5-year waiting period = ~13.64% combined).
  - Reserves constituted from 31 December 2025 onward fall under the new 9.8% regime (~18% combined, matching the new VVPRbis rate). An anti-abuse rule targets year-end fiscal-year manipulations.
- The uniform ~18% effective rate harmonises VVPRbis with liquidation reserves going forward.

**Consequences for a logopedist BV owner planning dividend distributions**:

1. **Act now for grandfathered distributions**: any VVPRbis dividend decided and effectively distributed before the effective date (targeted ~30 April 2026) locks in 15%. The AV must make a formal distribution decision and the dividend must be payable before the effective date.
2. **Liquidation reserves already built up** for fiscal years ending ≤ 30 December 2025 retain the favourable 6.5% rate after 5 years. Planning: continue to constitute liquidation reserves under the old regime wherever the closing date allows.
3. **Interim dividend** versus **dividend out of retained profits**: the form does not matter — what matters is the date of the AV decision and effective payment.
4. **Post-May 2026**: the effective rate on VVPRbis rises from 15% to 18% — a 3 percentage-point increase, or a ~20% relative increase in tax cost on each euro distributed.
5. **Halingo calculator**: must implement a date-sensitive toggle; dividend simulations for fiscal years 2025 and 2026 need to distinguish "distributed before X" vs "distributed after X" and default to a placeholder until the Staatsblad date is known.

## Item 4 — €25,000 → €30,000 BTW kleine-ondernemingsregeling threshold — RESOLVED

**Conclusion**: As of 2026-04-06 the threshold is **still €25,000**. The €30,000 proposal is in the government's programme (De Wever I) but has **NOT been voted** and is **NOT in the Staatsblad**. It is included as an intention in a draft programmawet but has not been enacted.

**Current state of the law**:
- Legal base: article **56bis WBTW** (Wetboek BTW), as lastly amended per wet van 23 November 2023 and the EU SME directive 2020/285 transposition effective 1 January 2025. These changes harmonised the cross-border small-business regime and maintained the threshold at **€25,000** (excl. VAT) per calendar year.
- FOD Financiën official page (reviewed 2026-04-06) still lists €25,000.

**Pending change (not yet enacted)**:
- De Wever I coalition agreement includes raising the threshold to €30,000 and adjusting the calculation method.
- Included in a draft programmawet but not yet voted and not yet in the Staatsblad.
- No transitional rules published.
- Until publication, €25,000 remains the legal threshold.

**Practical guidance for halingo**:
- Keep the product's default threshold configurable. For AJ 2026 / income year 2026, continue to use €25,000.
- When the law passes, the SaaS will need to (a) update the threshold parameter, (b) possibly recompute whether a user crosses the new threshold mid-year (usually the new threshold becomes effective from the start of the next calendar year to avoid mid-year disruptions), (c) surface a notification.

### Legal framework

- **KB van 13 juli 2017** tot vaststelling van de toelagen en vergoedingen van de personeelsleden van het federaal openbaar ambt — source for the kilometervergoeding for civil servants.
- Quarterly indexation system is applied since **1 October 2022** (previously annual), per KB amendment of 10 November 2022 (Staatsblad 16/11/2022).
- The indexation is calculated 80% on the evolution of the health index and 20% on the evolution of fuel prices over the preceding quarter.
- Fiscal transposition: FOD Financiën aligns by circulaire (e.g. **Circulaire 2023/C/78** of 18/09/2023, **Circulaire 2023/C/85**, **Circulaire 2024/C/X**, **Circulaire 2026/C/13** for Q1 2026), accepting the BOSA rate as a non-taxable maximum for private-sector employer reimbursements.
