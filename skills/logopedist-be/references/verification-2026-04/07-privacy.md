## Item 1 — Art. 34 Kwaliteitswet: electronic-only obligation for logopedists

**Status: RESOLVED.**

**Finding.** As of 2026-04-06, **no Royal Decree (KB/AR) has been taken** under art. 34 of the Kwaliteitswet (wet van 22 april 2019) to set an effective date — neither generally nor specifically for logopedists. Paper remains legally permissible.

**Exact text of art. 34 (consolidated on ejustice, Justel cn=2019042220):**

> "Vanaf een door de Koning bij een besluit vastgesteld na overleg in de Ministerraad te bepalen datum houdt de gezondheidszorgbeoefenaar het patiëntendossier bij en bewaart het in elektronische vorm."

The provision expressly authorises the King to fix **different dates for different categories of healthcare professions** ("De Koning kan hiervoor verschillende data bepalen voor verschillende categorieën van gezondheidszorgbeoefenaars"), so a logopedist-specific KB is the expected vehicle.

**Apparent contradiction in sources (resolved).** Several commentary pages (ASGB, Domus Medica) list "article 34" among the articles that entered into force on 1 January 2022 via the **KB van 12 december 2021** (BS 24 december 2021) on vervroegde inwerkingtreding. This is a numbering artefact: what those pages call "art. 34" in the 1 January 2022 bundle is the *patient-file content* provision of the original bill, which corresponds to art. 33 in the consolidated law (or art. 34 in an earlier draft numbering). The consolidated ejustice Justel text makes clear that the "electronic form" obligation in the **current** art. 34 still depends on a future KB that has not been published. Professional bodies (VVKP, Ordomedic, VVVVV) confirm in 2024-2025 communications that paper-only files remain lawful for paramedical professions including logopedists.

**Draft RD status.** No publicly notified draft RD for logopedists specifically, nor for paramedical professions in general, has been identified on ejustice.just.fgov.be or health.belgium.be as of 2026-04-06. FOD Volksgezondheid's Kwaliteitswet dossier pages still phrase art. 34 as "pending royal decree". No consultation documents tied to a concrete logopedist timeline were found.

**Practical consequence for halingo.** File 07 section 5 and section 17 ("Freshness notes") are correct: logopedists in April 2026 may keep paper, electronic or mixed files. Halingo may position electronic as best practice but MUST NOT claim a legal obligation specific to logopedists.

## Item 2 — "Sufficiently mature minor" — concrete age threshold?

**Status: RESOLVED.**

**Finding.** The 2024 reform (wet van 6 februari 2024, BS 29 februari 2024, in force 4 March 2024) did **not** introduce a statutory age threshold for the self-exercise of patient rights by a minor. Article 12 §2 still uses the *case-by-case* capacity test ("redelijke beoordeling van zijn belangen"), identical in substance to the original 2002 text.

**What the 2024 law actually changed about minors.**
- Art. 21 of the 2024 wijzigingswet modifies art. 12 §1 terminology (swapping "ouders" for "personen die ten aanzien van de minderjarige het ouderlijk gezag uitoefenen ... overeenkomstig boek I, titel IX van het oud Burgerlijk Wetboek").
- Introduces a post-mortem access regime for representatives of deceased minors (new art. 9 §4bis), subject to an override where the minor with sufficient capacity had refused such access in life.
- Strengthens the requirement to *involve* the minor in information and decision-making "rekening houdend met zijn leeftijd en maturiteit" — but still no fixed age.

**GBA position.** Advies nr. 15/2023 van 20 oktober 2023 (reviewed on the draft bill, ref. 152/2023) comments on the processing of personal data under the reform but does **not** propose or endorse a concrete age cutoff. It confirms the functional/competence-based approach. No post-2024 GBA decision has been identified that sets a bright-line age for logopedists or paramedics.

**Doctrine / sectoral guidance.**
- Ordomedic (Orde der artsen) advies "De arts en de minderjarigen" continues to cluster maturity around **12-14**, stressing individual assessment.
- Vlaamse Kinderrechtencommissariaat: functional approach, no bright line.
- Vlaams Artsensyndicaat and Domus Medica 2024 commentaries on the reform confirm that no statutory age was added.
- No *logopedist-specific* guidance exists; the general paramedical/WUG rule applies.

**Distinct threshold — do not confuse.** The GDPR art. 8 information-society-services consent age (set by art. 7 Kaderwet) is **13**. That is unrelated to the medical-treatment / patient-rights maturity test.

**Practical consequence for halingo.** File 07 section 9 is correct: treat it as a case-by-case test, cluster around 12-14 in product UX defaults, do **not** hardcode a single age, support a documented maturity assessment field, and keep the parents' rights active below ~12. No change needed to the file's substance.

**Sources.**
- Wet 6 februari 2024, etaamb.openjustice.be/nl/wet-van-06-februari-2024_n2024001224.html (full text of the amendment; art. 21 modifies art. 12).
- Wet 22 augustus 2002 Patiëntenrechten, Justel cn=2002082245 (consolidated art. 12 §2).
- GBA Advies nr. 15/2023 van 20 oktober 2023 (gegevensbeschermingsautoriteit.be/publications/advies-nr.-152-2023.pdf).

## Item 3 — Schrems III / DPF reliability as of 2026-04-06

**Status: RESOLVED.**

**Finding — current legal status of the DPF.**
- The EU-US Data Privacy Framework (Decision (EU) 2023/1795 of 10 July 2023) **remains in force** and valid as of 2026-04-06.
- On **3 September 2025**, in **Case T-553/23 Latombe v Commission**, the **General Court dismissed** the action for annulment of the DPF adequacy decision. The Court held that (i) the U.S. Data Protection Review Court provides sufficient independent redress, and (ii) bulk collection by U.S. signals intelligence is sufficiently circumscribed under Executive Order 14086.
- **Latombe has appealed** to the CJEU: the appeal was lodged on **31 October 2025** and is pending before the Court of Justice as **Case C-703/25 P** (reported in OJ C 2025/6610 and by Bird & Bird, WilmerHale, CMS-LawNow Dec 2025). No hearing or judgment yet.
- **NOYB / Max Schrems** has not filed a direct challenge to the DPF decision. Schrems's pending proceedings concern Meta's specific transfers (Irish High Court approval Feb 2024) and other controller-level matters, not the adequacy decision itself. A "Schrems III" in the narrow sense is therefore **still threatened but not pending at CJEU level** against the DPF as such — the pending CJEU case is Latombe, not Schrems.

**EDPB position.**
- EDPB's **first report under the DPF review mechanism** (adopted October 2024, ref. "EDPB adopts its first report under the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework") noted improvements (independent DPRC, new safeguards) but flagged **continuing concerns** around bulk-collection oversight and notification of data subjects. The EDPB did **not** recommend that controllers stop using the DPF.
- No EDPB opinion specifically prohibiting or discouraging DPF use for **health data**. EDPB Guidelines 03/2020 on health data remain the main health-specific document and pre-date the DPF.
- EDPB 2026 coordinated enforcement action (announced Oct 2025) focuses on Art. 13/14 transparency, not DPF reliability.

**GBA / APD position.**
- The GBA maintains an informational page "Adequaatheidsbeslissing voor gegevensoverdracht naar de Verenigde Staten" confirming the DPF is a valid transfer basis for certified US importers; the GBA points data subjects to the HHS/DPRC redress route for DPF-specific complaints.
- No GBA decision has been published that forbids, conditions, or discourages the use of US DPF-certified processors for Belgian **health data** specifically.
- However: GBA's 2024 Jaarverslag and 2026-2028 Strategic Plan (published 23 December 2025) re-emphasise health-data processing as a priority supervisory area, with particular attention to encryption, access control and sub-processor governance — not to DPF reliance per se.

**Sector-specific nuance.**
- The **eHealth Information Security Policy** baseline ("minimale normen" of KSZ-BCSS) and IVC deliberations continue to express a **strong preference for EU residency** of Belgian health data, without formally banning DPF-based US processing.
- Practical standard across Belgian healthcare SaaS in 2025-2026: EU region + EU-resident keys + TIA, with US DPF-certified sub-processors only for ancillary / non-clinical flows where unavoidable.

**Practical consequence for halingo.** File 07 section 12 is correct: treat the DPF as *currently valid* but architecturally non-load-bearing. Keep EU-region default, EU-resident customer-managed keys, and a public sub-processor list with mechanism per row. Monitor Case C-703/25 P Latombe and any later NOYB filings. If Latombe appeal reverses the General Court, the DPF would fall — architect so this is a configuration change, not a rebuild.

## Item 4 — Belgian NIS2 transposition law — citation, scope, applicability to halingo

**Status: RESOLVED.**

**Exact citation.**
**Wet van 26 april 2024 tot vaststelling van een kader voor de cyberbeveiliging van netwerk- en informatiesystemen van algemeen belang voor de openbare veiligheid** (FR: *Loi du 26 avril 2024 établissant un cadre pour la cybersécurité des réseaux et des systèmes d'information d'intérêt général pour la sécurité publique*).

- Published in the **Belgisch Staatsblad / Moniteur belge** on **17 May 2024**, numac **2024202344**.
- Entry into force: **18 October 2024** (aligned with the NIS2 Directive 2022/2555 transposition deadline).
- Implementing Royal Decree: **KB/AR van 9 juni 2024** setting out operational details (risk-management measures, incident reporting form and thresholds).
- Supersedes the prior NIS1 wet van 7 april 2019.

**Competent authority.** Centre for Cybersecurity Belgium (CCB / Centrum voor Cybersecurity België), via the **Safeonweb@work** platform.

**Registration deadline.** In-scope entities had to register with CCB by **18 March 2025** (5 months after entry into force). By **18 April 2025**, registered entities had to be able to demonstrate compliance with core risk-management obligations.

**Size thresholds (recommendation 2003/361/EC).**
- **Essential entity**: large enterprise (≥ 250 employees, OR annual turnover > €50 M AND balance sheet > €43 M) in a Bijlage I "very critical" sector.
- **Important entity**: medium-sized enterprise (50-249 employees OR €10-50 M turnover/balance sheet) in a Bijlage I sector; OR a medium/large enterprise in a Bijlage II "critical" sector.
- **Out of scope (default)**: micro/small enterprises — **< 50 employees AND both turnover AND balance sheet total < €10 M** (cumulative). Exceptions exist for DNS providers, TLD registries, trust service providers, public administrations, sole providers in an essential sector, and entities specifically designated by the competent authority.

**Health sector under NIS2.** "Gezondheidszorg" is in **Bijlage I (very critical)**. The Bijlage I healthcare sub-sectors are narrower than "every healthcare actor": healthcare providers as defined in Directive 2011/24/EU, EU reference laboratories, R&D of medicinal products, manufacturers of basic pharmaceutical products and preparations, manufacturers of critical medical devices (MDR art. 22). Belgian transposition follows these categories. **Individual logopedist solo/group practices are not automatically "essential" or "important" entities** — they are cross-border healthcare providers under Directive 2011/24/EU in principle, but the size thresholds exclude micro/small practices by default.

**SaaS providers — where does halingo fit?**
- Halingo is **not** itself a "healthcare provider" under Directive 2011/24/EU — it is an ICT service provider to healthcare providers.
- The relevant NIS2 scope categories for a SaaS like halingo are in **Bijlage II (critical, important by default)**:
  - "**Providers of managed services and managed security services**" (MSP/MSSP) — Annex I point 8, a Bijlage I very-critical sector. A multi-tenant practice-management SaaS with ongoing operational management for customers can qualify as an MSP.
  - "**Digital providers**" (Annex II point 6): online marketplaces, search engines, social networking platforms — unlikely to apply to halingo.
- **Size filter applies**: even if halingo qualifies as an MSP, it is out-of-scope by default if it remains a micro/small enterprise (< 50 employees AND < €10 M turnover AND < €10 M balance sheet).
- **Current reality for halingo (2026-04-06)**: "~100 praktijken" customer base and SME size → almost certainly **below the 50 employee / €10 M threshold**, so **not directly in NIS2 scope as an entity**. But this is a moving target as the company scales, and contractual flow-down obligations from NIS2-scope customers (e.g. a hospital using halingo) can impose supply-chain security requirements indirectly.

**Obligations if in scope.**
- Register with CCB on Safeonweb@work (name, KBO number, contacts, IP ranges, services).
- **Incident notification** (art. 23):
  - **Early warning** to CCB within **24 hours** of awareness of a significant incident.
  - **Incident notification** within **72 hours** (updated assessment, cross-border effects).
  - **Final report** within **1 month**.
- **Risk-management measures** (art. 21): policy on information security, incident handling, business continuity, supply-chain security, secure development, cryptography, access control, multi-factor authentication, awareness training, encryption.
- **Management body liability**: the management body (geschäftsführende organen) must approve and supervise the measures, may be held personally liable, and must follow cybersecurity training.
- **Penalties**: for essential entities up to **€10 M or 2%** of global annual turnover; for important entities up to **€7 M or 1.4%**.
- **Supply-chain security**: in-scope entities must assess the cybersecurity practices of their suppliers and sub-contractors, including software/SaaS providers — this is how halingo can be indirectly affected via its healthcare-provider customers.

**Practical consequence for halingo.** File 07 section 13 (breach notification / NIS2 paragraph) is substantially correct but should:
- Use the precise citation "wet van 26 april 2024, BS 17 mei 2024";
- Clarify that halingo as an SME (< 50 FTE, < €10 M) is **not directly in scope** today but supports NIS2 customers via contractual measures and the 24h/72h/1-month incident timeline;
- Note that scaling up (≥ 50 FTE) or being designated an MSP would pull halingo directly into scope;
- Add that registration is via **CCB Safeonweb@work**, not a separate breach portal.
