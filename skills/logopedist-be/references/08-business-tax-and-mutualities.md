## 1. Legal & tax status of the individual logopedist

A Belgian logopedist can exercise the profession under three main statutes:

1. As a **self-employed natural person** (eenmanszaak / indépendant en personne physique).
2. As an **employed logopedist** (loontrekkend / salarié) inside a hospital, rehabilitation centre, CLB/CPMS, MPI/CAR, school, VAPH-facility or private praktijk.
3. As a **self-employed freelancer collaborating with a praktijk** on a retrocession/commission basis — formally self-employed, but economically embedded in a host praktijk.

A BV/SRL (section 2) is a fourth dimension layered on top of (1) or (3): the individual logopedist remains the physical person providing care, while the vennootschap is the legal vehicle invoicing and owning the business assets.

### 1.1 Self-employed natural person (eenmanszaak)

**Setup steps** (a deliberately compact checklist; see Securex, Acerta, Liantis and UNIZO starter guides for full walkthroughs):

1. **KBO/BCE enrolment** (Kruispuntbank van Ondernemingen / Banque-Carrefour des Entreprises) via an erkend ondernemingsloket (Acerta, Liantis, Securex, Partena, Xerius, UCM). Activity code NACEBEL 86.909 "Activiteiten op het vlak van de menselijke gezondheidszorg" with a logopedie-specific sub-activity. Cost ~€109.10 in 2025 (indexed annually).
2. **RIZIV/INAMI identification number** ("M-nummer") — must have been obtained earlier through the recognition procedure (see document 04). The M-number is the foundation for conventioned billing and MyCareNet.
3. **Affiliation with a sociaal verzekeringsfonds voor zelfstandigen** (social insurance fund for the self-employed — RSVZ/INASTI framework). Must be done before starting activity and at the latest the day you start. The social fund calculates and collects sociale bijdragen and mediates with the RSVZ.
4. **VAT identification** (activation of the BTW number) if the activity includes any taxable component (see section 3). Many logopedists skip this initially and register later when they add a non-therapeutic activity.
5. **Business bank account** — legally mandatory for any professional activity (separated from private account), though the obligation is practical rather than a notarial precondition.
6. **Professional liability insurance** — not legally mandatory for logopedists as such, but strongly recommended and required by most collaboration contracts and by the VVL/UPLF professional associations.
7. **Mutuality** — as a self-employed person you remain insured for care under the compulsory health insurance via your chosen mutuality, but contributions are now integrated in the quarterly social contributions since the harmonisation of small vs. large risks in 2008.

**Income taxation — personenbelasting / impôt des personnes physiques (PB/IPP)**

Self-employed logopedists file a normal PB return and declare their net profit (winsten / bénéfices for commercants, or baten / profits for vrije beroepen — logopedists fall under "baten" because they are a vrij beroep). The net baten equal gross professional receipts minus professional expenses.

Progressive federal rates for income year 2025 (AJ 2026), unchanged in structure by the Di Rupo and De Wever governments:

| Bracket (net taxable income) | Federal rate |
|---|---|
| €0 – €15,820 | 25% |
| €15,820 – €27,920 | 40% |
| €27,920 – €48,320 | 45% |
| > €48,320 | 50% |

On top of the federal rate there is the gemeentebelasting (municipal surcharge, typically 6–9% of the federal tax) and in some cases a regional surcharge. The "belastingvrije som" (basic allowance) is €10,570 in AJ 2026.

**Professional expenses — forfait vs. real**

Self-employed persons may choose each year between:

- **Real expenses**: itemise and justify every cost (invoices, receipts). This is the almost-universal choice for established logopedists because their real costs (rent, software, materials, VAPZ, mutuality, car, depreciation) invariably exceed the forfait.
- **Wettelijk forfait (statutory lump sum)**: for profits (winsten) in 2025 the lump sum is **30% of gross profit, capped at €5,750**. For **vrije beroepen / baten** the lump sum uses a regressive percentage scale with a **maximum of €5,050** (income year 2025; verify annually at fin.belgium.be). Because a full-time logopedist easily spends more than €5,050 on rent, software, car costs and VAPZ alone, the forfait is typically only chosen in the very first partial year or by extreme-low-cost bijberoep profiles.

**Social contributions (sociale bijdragen)**

Self-employed persons in hoofdberoep owe 20.5% of their net taxable professional income from three years earlier, subject to a minimum (€890.42/quarter in 2026 for non-starters) and a maximum (€5,103.05/quarter in 2026). The provisional quarterly calls are based on income three years ago; a definitive correction (regularisatie) follows once the tax authority communicates the actual income. The fund also levies a beheersbijdrage (admin fee) of roughly 3.05%–4.20% on top, depending on fund and tier.

The three-tier ("drie kwartalen") system: every quarter a self-employed person receives a provisional call, with the possibility to voluntarily adjust upward or downward; any unpaid balance is reclaimed via regularisatie ~2 years after the year-end, when NAP/FSMA data is available.

**Starter / primostarter reduction**

A "primostarter" (someone starting as hoofdberoep self-employed who has not been hoofdberoep self-employed in the previous 20 quarters) pays reduced social contributions for the **first four quarters**:

- If estimated income stays below €8,972.07 (2026 reference floor), the calls are approximately **€341.30 in Q1 and €459.82 in Q2–Q4** (2026 figures per Xerius, Liantis). Plus 20.5% on any portion above the floor.
- Additionally, for 2026 a **one-off starterskorting** of about €123.26 is automatically deducted, per VLAIO.

The regularisatie still applies: if the actual first-year income turns out to be well above €8,972.07, the difference is later clawed back, though still at a reduced base.

**VAPZ / PLCI — Vrij Aanvullend Pensioen voor Zelfstandigen / Pension Libre Complémentaire Indépendants**

The single most important tax-advantaged pension vehicle for Belgian self-employed professionals. Contributions are 100% deductible as a professional expense, which means they reduce taxable income AND the base for future social contributions (the "double tax advantage"). There are two variants:

| Variant | Max contribution 2026 (legally in force) | Feature |
|---|---|---|
| Gewoon (ordinary) VAPZ | 8.17% of net taxable income three years earlier × indexation coefficient (676.50 / 630.85), capped at **€4,086.34** | Pure pension |
| Sociaal VAPZ | 9.4% × indexation, capped at **€4,701.54** | Also provides solidarity cover (incapacity, death, maternity supplement); legally required to earmark ≥10% to solidarity |

Note (verified 2026-04-06): the values above are the **legally applicable 2026 values** at publication time. The announced increase to **8.5% / 9.78%** with caps of **€4,251.39 / €4,891.60** was confirmed as **not yet voted and not yet in the Belgian State Gazette** — the law change is expected to pass in the course of 2026. Until publication, contributions must respect 8.17% / 9.4% with caps €4,086.34 / €4,701.54. Once published, therapists can top up the difference retroactively within the year. Formula base: net belastbaar beroepsinkomen 2023 (AJ 2024) × 676.50 / 630.85 × percentage, with absolute cap.

For a solo-praktijk logopedist with a net baten of €45,000, maxing out the VAPZ is almost always the right default because it saves ~50% marginal tax rate plus ~20% social contribution — roughly €3,200 in immediate savings on a €4,250 contribution.

### 1.2 Employed logopedist (loontrekkend)

Most employed logopedists work in:

- **Ziekenhuizen / hôpitaux** (general, psychiatric, university);
- **Revalidatiecentra / centres de revalidation** (RIZIV-conventioned);
- **MPI / CAR / ZML** for children with disabilities;
- **CLB / CPMS** (pupil-guidance centres);
- **Woonzorgcentra / MRPA** (very rarely as primary employer);
- **Private praktijken** with an employment contract rather than a freelance arrangement.

**Applicable paritair comité / commission paritaire**

The private healthcare sector is primarily covered by **Paritair Comité 330 – Paritair Comité voor de gezondheidsinrichtingen en -diensten** (CP 330 – Commission paritaire des établissements et des services de santé). PC 330 encompasses hospitals, rehabilitation centres, home nursing, elderly care and private paramedical collaborations. Logopedists employed in hospitals, MPIs and CARs fall under PC 330. Wage scales (loonbarema's) are fixed by function category and seniority and indexed twice a year or at fixed triggers.

For logopedists employed by a **private solo/group praktijk that is not itself hospital-affiliated**, the authoritative answer (verified 2026-04-06) is **PC 330.04** — the subsector "Gezondheidsinrichtingen en -diensten — overige" (the "andere" / residual healthcare subsector). The official scope of PC 330.04 explicitly lists "**de kabinetten van huisartsen, specialisten, kinesitherapeuten, tandartsen en andere paramedici**". Because logopedists are a legally recognised paramedical profession under the KB of 20 October 1994, private logopedie cabinets fall under "andere paramedici" in this enumeration. PC 200 (aanvullend paritair comité voor bedienden) is strictly residual and does NOT apply when a sectoral PC is competent, so PC 200 is not the right PC for a pure ambulant private logopedie praktijk. The correct classification must still be confirmed per employer with the social secretariat (Acerta, Securex, SD Worx, Liantis, Partena) based on the dominant NACEBEL code in the KBO/BCE — but the default answer is PC 330.04. Multi-disciplinary praktijken follow the dominant activity; a praktijk BV that also holds real estate stays in PC 330.04 if its operational activity is paramedical. Freelancers are not covered by any PC (PCs only bind employees).

**Key employment clauses relevant to SaaS design**

- **Non-compete**: strict limitations under Belgian labour law. Must be limited in time (max 12 months), geography, and activity; must provide financial compensation at termination; otherwise void.
- **Confidentiality** — standard, reinforced for healthcare by GDPR + beroepsgeheim (criminal code art. 458).
- **Volledige of deeltijdse arbeidsovereenkomst** with a working regime (typically 38h/week for full-time in PC 330).
- **IFIC** — the functieclassificatie IFIC (Instituut voor Functieclassificatie / Institut de Classification de Fonctions) is binding in many PC 330 subsectors since 2018 and assigns each function to a category with a corresponding wage scale.

### 1.3 Freelance / commission model inside a praktijk — and the schijnzelfstandigheid risk

This is the dominant growth model in Belgian private logopedie since ~2015 and the segment halingo.be is most likely to serve.

**How it typically works in practice**

- A praktijk BV (or eenmanszaak) owns/leases treatment rooms, handles reception, manages waiting lists, runs the MyCareNet gateway and pays for software/marketing/materials.
- The freelance logopedist is a self-employed natural person (with their own M-number, KBO/BCE registration, social fund and, optionally, VAT number).
- The freelancer invoices the patients directly (or, more commonly, the praktijk invoices/eAttests the patients on behalf of the freelancer, then passes the gross fees to the freelancer minus a retrocession). Both legal structures exist; the tax treatment differs materially.
- The praktijk retains a **retrocession percentage** ("commission", "afhouding", "management fee") in exchange for infrastructure and support.

**Typical retrocession splits (market observation, not regulated)**

Sources: public anecdotes from praktijk-recruitment adverts, Liantis/Acerta freelance guides, and internal conversations on forums like the VVL/UPLF Facebook groups. None of this is fixed by law; the percentages vary widely.

| Praktijk profile | Typical retrocession (praktijk share) | Freelancer keeps |
|---|---|---|
| Basic room rental, no admin support | 15%–20% | 80%–85% |
| Room + reception + software + MyCareNet | 25%–35% | 65%–75% |
| Room + full back-office + marketing + patient intake + supervision | 35%–50% | 50%–65% |
| Full "franchise" model with branding and continuing education | up to 50%–55% | 45%–50% |

The most commonly cited middle-of-the-road splits are **25%/75%, 30%/70% and 35%/65%**, with the freelancer keeping the larger share.

**Schijnzelfstandigheid — faux indépendant risk**

The legal framework is the **Arbeidsrelatieswet** (wet van 27 december 2006 houdende diverse bepalingen (IV), Titel XIII), which set four general criteria used by the RSZ/ONSS and the courts to distinguish an employment contract from a self-employment contract:

1. **Wil van de partijen** (parties' will, as expressed in writing);
2. **Vrijheid van organisatie van de werktijd** (freedom to organise working time);
3. **Vrijheid van organisatie van het werk** (freedom to organise the work);
4. **Mogelijkheid om een hiërarchische controle uit te oefenen** (the possibility of hierarchical control).

When at least three of four point to subordination, the qualification as self-employed can be overturned by the RSZ, resulting in a reclassification as employee, retroactive payment of employer and employee social contributions (with interest and penalties) and possible labour-court action. The law provides for sector-specific lists of additional, neutral and rebuttable indicators in certain "at-risk" sectors (construction, security, transport, cleaning, agriculture) — **healthcare is currently NOT a listed sector**, so the general four-criteria test applies.

**Red flags** that in practice have caused reclassification in the healthcare sector:

- Fixed schedule imposed by the praktijk (the freelancer cannot freely pick their days/hours);
- Obligation to be present in specific rooms at specific times;
- Mandatory attendance at team meetings, continuing education or weekend duties;
- Uniform or dress-code imposition;
- Inability to refuse a patient assigned by the praktijk;
- Freelancer working exclusively for one praktijk (no other clients);
- Fixed monthly minimum remuneration regardless of turnover;
- The praktijk invoices the patient in its own name and the freelancer is paid a variable amount that functions like a commission rather than a fee.

**Green flags** that strengthen the self-employed qualification:

- Written freelance-collaboration agreement clearly stating each party's autonomy;
- Freelancer has own KBO/BCE number, own professional insurance and own patient list;
- Freelancer has multiple clients/praktijken or private patients outside the praktijk;
- Freelancer decides their own calendar and blocks time freely;
- Retrocession is calculated as a transparent percentage of turnover, not a fixed salary;
- Freelancer bears commercial risk (no patients = no income);
- Freelancer invoices the praktijk (or the praktijk invoices in the freelancer's name with clear per-session/per-patient traceability).

The **Commissie ter regeling van de arbeidsrelatie** (Commission Arbeidsrelaties / Commission Relations de Travail) offers a binding ruling on qualification upon request by either party — a useful risk-management tool for praktijken building a large freelance pool.

**Invoicing direction and VAT interaction**

Two legal constructions are common:

1. **Retrocession of fees (retrocessie van erelonen)**: the freelancer is the legal provider of care to the patient; the patient pays the freelancer (or the praktijk collects on behalf); the freelancer then retrocedes the agreed percentage to the praktijk as compensation for services received (rent, admin, software, marketing). The retrocession is considered a **taxable supply of services by the praktijk to the freelancer** and is in principle **subject to 21% VAT** (because it is NOT a medical care service; it is a B2B service of room rental + administration). This can be a cost for the freelancer because they cannot (fully) deduct the VAT if they are exempt under article 44 WBTW.

2. **Therapist as sub-contractor of the praktijk (onderaanneming)**: the praktijk is the contractual care provider to the patient; the freelancer sub-contracts their therapeutic act to the praktijk. In this direction, the freelancer invoices the praktijk for therapeutic services — which, if therapeutic in purpose, are VAT-exempt under article 44 §1 WBTW (see section 3). No VAT flows at all.

The **direction of the invoicing flow therefore determines VAT liability**. A praktijk BV that charges a pure "cost-sharing" fee may try to benefit from the article 44 §1bis WBTW exemption for "zelfstandige groeperingen van personen" (cost-sharing association / kostendelende vereniging), which exempts certain shared-infrastructure services between paramedical members if strict conditions are met — in practice rarely usable because of the "absence of distortion of competition" condition and the administrative burden (separate KBO structure, mandatory member-only supply, etc.).

**Halingo product implication**: the SaaS must support both invoicing directions per collaboration. This is load-bearing for the freelance module.

## 2. Business entity structures

### 2.1 BV / SRL — Besloten Vennootschap / Société à Responsabilité Limitée

Since the entry into force of the **Wetboek van Vennootschappen en Verenigingen (WVV — law of 23 March 2019, Belgian Official Gazette 4 April 2019)** on 1 May 2019, the Belgian corporate landscape has been radically simplified: from ~17 legal forms down to 4 main forms (BV, NV, CV, maatschap and partnerships), plus VZW (ASBL) for non-profits. The pre-existing BVBA was automatically rebranded as BV.

**Why a logopedist might incorporate**

1. **Liability limitation** — in principle the BV's debts do not reach the shareholder's private patrimony (subject to founder liability for manifestly insufficient initial capital during the first three years — see below).
2. **Tax optimisation** — at income levels above roughly €60,000–€70,000 net baten, the combined personenbelasting + sociale bijdragen bite becomes heavier than corporate-tax + manager-remuneration + optional dividend. The exact break-even depends on family situation, child allowances, mortgage and the VAPZ/PLCI deposit.
3. **Succession / asset planning** — shares are easier to transfer than a practice as a going concern; family transfer through share donation benefits from favourable gift-tax rates.
4. **Separation of private and professional patrimony** — especially relevant when investing in treatment-room real estate.
5. **Hiring and contracting leverage** — it is much easier to hire employees or contract freelancers through a BV than through an eenmanszaak (although legally both are possible).

**No minimum capital — but adequate initial capital ("toereikend aanvangsvermogen")**

Under the WVV (art. 5:3 and 5:4), the old €18,550 minimum capital for the BVBA is abolished. The BV no longer has a "capital" concept at all. Instead, founders must put together a **toereikend aanvangsvermogen** — initial equity "adequate in light of the intended activity" — and substantiate this in a **financial plan** delivered to the notary before incorporation.

The financial plan must cover **at least two years of projected operations** (art. 5:4 §2 WVV) and describe revenue forecasts, expected costs, financing sources and working-capital needs.

**Founder liability risk** (art. 5:16 WVV): if the company is declared bankrupt within three years of incorporation and it appears that the initial capital was manifestly insufficient, the founders are held personally and jointly liable for the company's debts up to what would have been needed. Verdict: do not "under-capitalise" a BV just because the minimum is abolished; build a realistic starting buffer (€5,000–€20,000 is typical for a solo-praktijk BV).

**Corporate tax — vennootschapsbelasting / impôt des sociétés**

Rates as of AJ 2026 (income year 2025):

| Tier | Rate |
|---|---|
| First €100,000 of taxable profit (KMO reduced rate) | **20%** |
| Profit above €100,000 (or non-KMO) | **25%** |

**Conditions to benefit from the 20% reduced rate (art. 215 WIB92)**:

1. The company qualifies as a **kleine vennootschap** under art. 1:24 WVV: not exceeding more than one of the three thresholds over the last two financial years — (a) balance sheet total €6,000,000, (b) turnover (excl. VAT) €11,250,000, (c) annual average 50 FTE.
2. The company pays its director/manager a **minimum remuneration of €50,000/year** (raised from €45,000 in budget law 2024/2025) or equal to the taxable profit if profit is below €100,000. **Maximum 20% of this €50,000 may consist of benefits in kind** (e.g. a company car).
3. The company is not a financial company, not majority held by other companies (> 50%), and does not distribute dividends in excess of 13% of paid-up capital.
4. It is not a "startersvennootschap" exception (see below).

**Exception for startups**: newly founded small companies are exempted from the €50,000 minimum-remuneration condition during their **first four financial years** (art. 215 § 3 WIB92). This is important because a first-year praktijk BV rarely generates enough margin to carry a €50,000 manager salary.

**Dividend vs. salary — the VVPRbis regime**

Dividends paid to a natural-person shareholder are subject to a **30% roerende voorheffing (withholding tax)** as the default. The **VVPRbis** regime (art. 269 §2 WIB92) reduces this to:

- 20% for dividends paid from the profits of the second fiscal year after incorporation/capital increase (cash contribution only);
- **15% from the third fiscal year onward** — the target rate.

**Conditions for VVPRbis**:

1. Shares issued after 1 July 2013 in exchange for a cash contribution;
2. Small-company status at the time of the capital increase;
3. Shares held in full ownership continuously since issuance;
4. No preference over other shares for dividends or liquidation proceeds;
5. The cash must be fully paid up.

**Changes announced in 2025/2026 (De Wever government) — verified 2026-04-06**: the November 2025 budget agreement announced raising the reduced rate from 15% to **18%** for VVPRbis dividends and liquidation reserves, as well as abolishing the intermediate 20% tier. The draft law is **Wetsontwerp programmawet DOC 56-1378/001** (Kamer 2025-2026), deposited 23 February 2026. The final vote was scheduled for 26 March 2026 but was postponed past 1 April 2026 pending NAR advice; **as of 2026-04-06 the programmawet has NOT been voted and NOT been published in the Belgian State Gazette**. Entry into force is on the first day of the month following publication — earliest realistic effective date **1 May 2026**. **Transition rules** (as currently drafted): (a) NO transition regime for VVPRbis dividends — all distributions paid from the effective date are taxed at 18% regardless of when reserves were built up; small companies can therefore still distribute VVPRbis dividends at 15% up to and including 30 April 2026; (b) liquidation reserves: reserves constituted through 30 December 2025 retain the old 6.5% rate after a 5-year waiting period (~13.64% combined), reserves constituted from 31 December 2025 onward fall under the new 9.8% rate (~18% combined, with an anti-abuse rule targeting fiscal-year manipulation). **Consequence for a BV-owning logopedist**: any VVPRbis dividend decided by the AV and payable before the effective date locks in 15%; post-effective date the rate becomes 18% (a ~20% relative increase in dividend tax cost). Halingo dividend simulators must implement a date-sensitive toggle.

**Liquidation reserve** — an alternative to VVPRbis: a company can annually earmark part of its profit as "liquidatiereserve" against an immediate 10% anticipated tax; after a **5-year waiting period** the reserve can be distributed to the shareholder at a further 5% withholding (currently; also set to increase under the 2025/2026 reform), for a combined ~13.64% effective rate. Often the best alternative if VVPRbis conditions are not met.

**Management fees between praktijk BV and a "manager BV"**

A frequent tax-optimisation structure: the logopedist incorporates a **second BV (manager BV)** that owns shares in the **operational praktijk BV** and invoices management fees to it. This allows the logopedist's income to be routed through two layers of corporate tax (instead of directly as director remuneration at personal-income rates). Benefits: further income smoothing, more flexibility on dividend timing, separation of personal reputation risk. Risks: the **"simulation" or "abuse of tax law" doctrine** (art. 344 WIB92) can attack structures that lack any non-tax motive, and the FOD Financiën has targeted manager BVs in healthcare in several rulings. A genuine manager-BV structure must have business substance (real services performed, arm's length fees, proper contracts).

**Kaaimantax and other anti-abuse rules**

The **kaaimantax** (art. 5/1 WIB92) targets Belgian residents who hold wealth in low-tax juridical structures abroad (trusts, foundations, certain foreign companies) by taxing the income directly in the hand of the founder/beneficiary ("look-through taxation"). For a pure domestic logopedie BV this is irrelevant, but a praktijk BV with foreign holdings (e.g. a Luxembourg or Cyprus entity holding IP) would trigger it. For halingo's target audience this is an edge case; flag but do not design for it.

Other relevant anti-abuse rules: **art. 344 §1 WIB92** (general anti-abuse rule), **controlled foreign company rules** (not relevant for solo praktijken), **transfer-pricing obligations** (only for groups).

### 2.2 Maatschap / société simple and other forms

The **maatschap** (société simple) is regulated by art. 4:1 et seq. WVV. It is a contractual partnership without legal personality, no capital requirement, and no mandatory registration with the KBO if it does not pursue a commercial/profit-making activity of its own. Since the WVV, maatschappen that pursue an economic activity (and are therefore "ondernemingen") must register with the KBO/BCE.

**Use case for logopedists**: the **kostenmaatschap** (cost-sharing partnership) is the most relevant variant. Two or more self-employed logopedists share premises, reception staff, software, and other overhead through a maatschap that simply collects contributions and pays invoices. Each logopedist keeps their own patient relationship, own M-number, own income, own taxation. The maatschap itself is **tax-transparent**: it does not file its own tax return; each partner declares their share of the costs directly in their PB return.

**Advantages**:

- No notarial deed, no minimum capital, low setup cost;
- Preserves full self-employed status of each member;
- Strong protection against schijnzelfstandigheid (if structured cleanly);
- Simple cost allocation.

**Disadvantages**:

- **Unlimited joint and several liability** of all maten for debts of the maatschap;
- No legal personality — cannot own property in its own name;
- Partners remain individually liable towards landlords, software vendors, etc.

**Other forms briefly mentioned**:

- **CommV / SComm** (Commanditaire Vennootschap / Société en Commandite) — a partnership with active and silent partners; rarely used in healthcare.
- **NV / SA** (Naamloze Vennootschap / Société Anonyme) — overkill for a logopedie praktijk.
- **CV / SC** (Coöperatieve Vennootschap / Société Coopérative) — under the WVV strictly reserved for entities pursuing a **cooperative objective** (art. 6:1 WVV). Some logopedie collectives use it.
- **VZW / ASBL** — non-profit structure used by rehabilitation centres, MPI/CAR, patient associations — not directly an option for a for-profit private praktijk.

### 2.3 Mixed constructions

The typical mature private praktijk looks like this:

- **Praktijk BV** owns the lease (or the building via a separate real-estate BV), employs reception/admin staff, subscribes to halingo.be / other software, buys therapy materials, handles billing via MyCareNet.
- The **founder-logopedist** is a bedrijfsleider (zaakvoerder / gérant) of the BV, takes a monthly salary of at least €50,000/year (to preserve the reduced corporate tax rate), optionally tops up with VVPRbis dividends annually.
- The BV **employs one or two junior logopedists** on a PC 330 employment contract for entry-level staff.
- The BV **contracts 3–10 freelance logopedists** on a retrocession model, with per-month invoicing.
- Some praktijken additionally run a **management BV** above the praktijk BV, holding the shares and invoicing management fees.

**Accounting implications**:

- The praktijk BV accounts for gross patient fees (for staff logopedists and employed hours), retrocession-percentages withheld from freelancer turnover (as room-rental + services income, either VAT-taxable or under kostendelende vereniging), employment costs, rent, depreciation, management fee to the parent, VAPZ/DPLI for the director, etc.
- The manager BV declares management-fee income from its subsidiary, pays its own 20%/25% corporate tax, pays the director a €50,000+ salary, distributes dividends under VVPRbis.
- Freelancers maintain their own single-entry books, declare net baten in PB, pay social contributions and VAPZ individually.

**Halingo product implication**: the multi-entity reality is a first-class requirement. The product must support a praktijk "organisation" with multiple therapist "profiles", each with its own employment/freelance status, retrocession percentage, and VAT regime, and roll up revenue by therapist for the pay-out / retrocession calculation.

## 3. VAT (BTW / TVA) for logopedists

### 3.1 The medical exemption (art. 44 §1 WBTW) and the 2022 reform

**Pre-2022 situation**: art. 44 §1 WBTW exempted "the services performed by doctors, dentists, kinesitherapists, midwives, nurses, care providers, accredited paramedical practitioners" from VAT — a broad profession-based exemption. Logopedists fell under this umbrella as an officially recognised paramedical profession (KB of 20 October 1994 recognising the profession).

**The 2022 reform**: the **wet van 11 juli 2021** (Belgian Official Gazette 20 July 2021) amended art. 44 §1 WBTW with effect from **1 January 2022**. The amendment was triggered by Constitutional Court judgment n° 194/2019 of 5 December 2019, which found that the old broad exemption violated the principle of equality (and EU VAT Directive jurisprudence — the Court of Justice of the EU had repeatedly held that the medical VAT exemption must be limited to services with a therapeutic purpose).

**The new rule** (art. 44 §1 WBTW, as amended):

1. The exemption is **limited to services with a therapeutic purpose** — diagnosis, treatment and, insofar as possible, cure of diseases or health problems.
2. The exemption extends to practitioners holding a legally regulated healthcare profession title AND to practitioners who, while not holding a regulated title, possess "adequate training" of equivalent quality — but the latter must make a prior notification to the FOD Financiën.
3. Services that are **not therapeutic in purpose** (aesthetic, expert-witness, media, coaching, commercial) become subject to VAT at **21%** (or the applicable rate if a reduced rate applies).

**The implementing circular** is **Circulaire 2021/C/114 of 20 December 2021**, followed by updates in **Circulaire 2022/C/4** and **Circulaire 2022/C/61** (together the "FAQ package" on the reform).

**Application to logopedists specifically**

Logopedists are explicitly listed as a recognised paramedical profession qualifying for the therapeutic exemption. The circular introduces a **rebuttable presumption of therapeutic purpose** for acts that:

- Appear in the **RIZIV nomenclature** of logopedic services (see document 01);
- Are performed in the framework of the revalidatiewetgeving (VAPH-agreements, etc.);
- Are reimbursed by the verplichte ziekteverzekering.

In practice, the vast majority of daily logopedie acts fall under the exemption because they target:

- Speech-production disorders (articulatie, fonologie, stotteren);
- Language-development disorders (SLI, dysphasia);
- Learning disorders (dyslexia, dysorthographia, dyscalculia);
- Voice disorders (dysphonia);
- Oral-motor disorders;
- Dysphagia / slikstoornissen;
- Aphasia and dysarthria (neurological);
- Hearing-rehabilitation logopedie for cochlear implants and hearing aids.

**Acts that are NOT therapeutic (and therefore 21% VAT applicable)**:

- **Expert reports for insurers, courts, employers or government agencies** (medico-legal reports, fitness-for-work assessments);
- **Communication coaching, public-speaking training, accent reduction** for non-pathological clients (purely professional or performance-focused);
- **Teaching/lecturing** at a higher education institution or continuing-education provider (although teaching can have its own exemption under art. 44 §2 4° WBTW — "onderwijs" — if given by an official educational institution);
- **Writing books, course materials, training modules** for commercial sale;
- **Media appearances, commercial endorsements**;
- **Screenings of entirely healthy populations** as commercial activity (not covered by a preventive health programme of the Gemeenschappen / Communautés);
- **Corporate voice/communication training** for companies;
- **Sale of goods** (see 3.3).

**Opt-in / declaration to remain under the exemption**: logopedists whose activities are entirely or predominantly therapeutic do NOT have to take any special step — they simply remain exempt under art. 44 §1. However, if the practitioner wishes to invoke the "therapeutic training equivalence" route (non-regulated titles), a formal **notification to the FOD Financiën** is required via a specific form. This is more relevant for non-regulated wellness professions (e.g. osteopaths, some psychotherapists) than for logopedists, who already hold a regulated profession title.

**Screenings and reports — a grey zone**: the circular explicitly addresses screening activities. Screenings performed as part of an **approved preventive health programme** (e.g. Kind & Gezin, ONE screenings for speech/language) remain exempt because they have a preventive/therapeutic collective purpose. Screenings performed commercially outside such a framework are taxable. Reports written for the patient's treating physician are exempt as a continuation of the therapeutic relationship; reports written for an insurer to justify a claim are taxable (they serve the insurer's evidentiary purpose, not the patient's therapy).

### 3.2 When a logopedist must register for VAT

A logopedist becomes a **btw-plichtige** (VAT-taxable person) as soon as they perform any activity falling outside the art. 44 §1 exemption, regardless of amount — the moment the first €1 of non-therapeutic turnover appears.

The most common triggers in the logopedie market:

- Giving training courses or supervision to colleagues for a fee (unless covered by the onderwijs-vrijstelling);
- Writing medico-legal expertises for insurance companies;
- Selling therapy materials, books or online modules;
- B2B language/communication consulting for companies;
- Speech coaching for actors, singers, public speakers where the purpose is not therapeutic;
- Freelance teaching at a university college (**art. 44 §2 4° exemption** may cover this; case by case);
- Writing for professional publications where a fee is received.

**The "vrijstellingsregeling kleine onderneming" (small-business exemption)** — art. 56bis WBTW

A mixed-activity logopedist whose **non-exempt turnover stays below €25,000 per calendar year (excl. VAT)** can opt for the **vrijstellingsregeling kleine onderneming**: they register for VAT but do not charge VAT on their non-exempt services and cannot deduct input VAT either. Administrative burden: quarterly VAT listings, annual turnover declaration by 31 March (art. 53quinquies WBTW).

**Important caveat**: the €25,000 threshold applies to the **non-exempt turnover only** — therapeutic fees exempt under art. 44 §1 do not count toward the €25,000 ceiling. So a full-time logopedist who does €70,000 in therapy + €5,000 in non-therapeutic coaching can still use the vrijstellingsregeling for the €5,000 coaching component.

**Planned increase (De Wever I government) — verified 2026-04-06**: the Arizona-coalition programme includes a proposal to raise the threshold from €25,000 to **€30,000** and to adjust the calculation method. The proposal is included in a draft programmawet but has **not yet been voted and is not in the Staatsblad** as of 2026-04-06. The legal threshold therefore remains **€25,000**. The FOD Financiën official guidance page still lists €25,000 at time of verification. Track in the Belgian State Gazette.

**Gemengde belastingplichtige / assujetti mixte**

A logopedist who registers for VAT to tax the non-therapeutic component becomes a **gemengde belastingplichtige** (mixed taxable person — art. 46 WBTW). They:

- Charge 21% (or applicable rate) on taxable activities;
- Do NOT charge VAT on exempt therapeutic activities;
- Can deduct input VAT only on the proportion of expenses used for taxable activities.

**Two methods to apportion input VAT deduction**:

1. **Algemeen verhoudingsgetal (general prorata)** — the default: deduct input VAT in proportion to (taxable turnover) / (total turnover). Simple to calculate, but distributes deduction across all expenses blindly (including expenses purely linked to therapy).

2. **Werkelijk gebruik (actual use)** — optional but requires **prior notification** to the FOD Financiën (mandatory since the reform implemented by KB 26 October 2022, effective 1 January 2023, and subsequently tightened by the circular of 30 October 2024 / Circulaire 2024/C/81). Under actual use, input VAT is allocated expense by expense: 100% deductible if used exclusively for the taxable activity, 0% if exclusively for the exempt activity, and a reasonable prorata for mixed expenses. **Actual use is almost always more favourable** for a mostly-therapeutic logopedist with a small taxable side-activity, because general prorata would allow only a tiny fraction of deduction on everything.

**Notification and reporting obligations for actual use**:

- Prior **electronic notification** via e-604A/B for new registrations, or via MyMinfin for existing ones;
- The notification must be made before the start of the first VAT period in which actual use is applied;
- Annual reporting of the applied prorata in the VAT declaration (box 63 / periodic declaration + annexes);
- Additional data reporting on the number of sectors, the applied apportionment keys, the input VAT amounts per sector — per Circulaire 2024/C/81, with tolerances introduced in October 2025 (see Practicali and BlogITAA October 2025 articles).

**Halingo product implication**: the SaaS must support a per-therapist VAT regime with three settings: (a) fully exempt (art. 44 §1, no VAT number), (b) mixed with vrijstellingsregeling kleine onderneming (VAT number, no VAT charged, no input deduction), (c) mixed belastingplichtige with real VAT (charged on non-therapeutic services, input deduction via prorata or actual use).

### 3.3 Sales of goods to patients

Logopedists frequently sell ancillary goods to patients as part of home exercise programs: therapy cards, exercise booklets, articulation games, picture cards, digital licences, online course access, articulation toys, oral-motor tools. **These sales are NOT covered by the medical exemption of art. 44 §1 WBTW** — they are supplies of goods, not services of therapeutic care. They are therefore **taxable** at the applicable VAT rate.

| Product | Applicable VAT rate | Legal basis |
|---|---|---|
| Printed books (including therapy workbooks in physical form) | **6%** | Annex table A, cat. XIX WBTW / KB n° 20 table A |
| E-books, digital publications supplied electronically | **6%** since 2019 reform (EU alignment) | KB n° 20 table A, following EU Directive 2018/1713 |
| Online course / digital exercise subscription sold to individuals | **21% in principle**, but 6% if it qualifies as an "electronic publication" equivalent to a book; case-by-case | KB n° 20 table A and FOD Financiën rulings |
| Therapy cards, picture cards as a pedagogical product | Usually **21%** unless they qualify as a book (bound, ISBN, etc.) | Standaardtarief |
| Educational toys, games, articulation props | **21%** | Standaardtarief |
| Oral-motor therapy tools (blowers, chewing tools) | **21%** | Standaardtarief (medical-device exemption does not apply — these are consumer tools, not registered medical devices) |
| Hearing aids, tactile aids for hearing impaired (registered medical device) | **6%** if listed in KB n° 20 table A, cat. XXIII | Reduced rate for medical devices |
| Software licences for therapeutic apps (B2C) | **21%** | Standaardtarief |

**The sale of goods triggers VAT registration** regardless of the amount (unless the vrijstellingsregeling kleine onderneming covers it). A logopedist who sells a €25 workbook to a patient and is currently fully exempt must decide:

1. **Stay under €25,000**: register for VAT but opt for vrijstellingsregeling — no VAT on the workbook, no deduction;
2. **Become belastingplichtige**: charge 21% on the workbook, become gemengde belastingplichtige, deduct input VAT on related purchases.

Most solo praktijken stay in the vrijstellingsregeling kleine onderneming because the volume is too low to justify the admin burden of quarterly VAT declarations.

**Registered cash register (GKS / SCE) obligation**

The **geregistreerd kassasysteem** (GKS, "witte kassa") obligation — KB of 30 December 2009 and subsequent texts — applies to the **horeca sector** (restaurants, cafés) whose food/beverage turnover exceeds €25,000/year. **It does NOT apply to logopedie praktijken**. Healthcare services exempt under art. 44 §1 fall outside the scope entirely. Even retail sales of therapy goods do not trigger the GKS — they are normal retail, which has its own invoicing rules but no GKS.

**Daily receipts obligation**

For sales directly to consumers without an invoice, the logopedist must keep a **dagboek van ontvangsten** (daily receipts journal) and, on request, issue a cash receipt. In practice, because most logopedie transactions are billed via eAttest or paper certificate (getuigschrift van verstrekte hulp), the goods sale is often added as a separate line on the praktijk's **separate commercial invoice**.

**Separation of care revenue and goods revenue — mandatory**

From an accounting and compliance standpoint the two revenue streams must be clearly separated:

- **Care revenue** — tracked with the patient's identity, the RIZIV nomenclature code, the mutuality if any, the applicable tariff (conventioneel / not), the eAttest transaction;
- **Goods revenue** — tracked as a separate stream, with product code, quantity, VAT rate (if any), and the normal sales invoice fields.

The tax authority expects the accounting to show the two separately so that the scope of the art. 44 exemption (services only) can be checked against the taxable sales (goods).

### 3.4 Rental, retrocession, inter-praktijk VAT

**Rental of treatment rooms by a praktijk BV to a freelance logopedist**

The default rule (art. 44 §3 2° WBTW) is that rental of immovable property is **VAT-exempt**. This is unfavourable for the lessor because they cannot deduct input VAT on construction/renovation/utilities.

**Optional VAT on professional real-estate rental — reform of 1 January 2019** (wet van 14 oktober 2018)

Since 1 January 2019, a lessor and a lessee can **jointly opt to subject the rental of a newly-built or newly-renovated professional building to 21% VAT** (art. 44 §3 2° d) WBTW). Conditions:

- **New construction** — the VAT applicable to the building construction must have been incurred from 1 October 2018 onward (fundamentally new building); renovation does not qualify unless it creates a "new" building from a VAT perspective;
- **Professional use** — the lessee uses the property exclusively in their economic activity;
- **Joint written declaration** of the option by both parties;
- **15-year adjustment period** for input VAT deduction.

For an established logopedie praktijk in an older building: **not applicable**, rental stays exempt. For a newly-built professional campus: the option can be valuable because the VAT on the construction is recoverable, but **the lessee must be able to deduct the 21% VAT**, which a fully-exempt logopedist cannot. Hence the option is almost always refused in logopedie contexts.

**Short-term rental (< 6 months)**: automatically subject to 21% VAT without the option — rarely relevant.

**Retrocession fees between freelance and praktijk (VAT treatment)**

Two invoicing directions, already introduced in section 1.3:

- **Praktijk → freelance** (room + admin + software invoice to the freelancer): the praktijk supplies a bundle of non-therapeutic services; **21% VAT in principle applies**, making the freelancer worse off if they are exempt. Some praktijken disaggregate: pure "room rental" component exempt (art. 44 §3 2°), "admin/software/reception" component 21%. Tax authority increasingly contests pure-rental-only labels as artificial.
- **Freelance → praktijk** (freelancer invoices their therapeutic service to the praktijk, which then bills the patient): the freelancer supplies a therapeutic service to the praktijk; if the praktijk uses it to continue delivering a therapeutic service to the patient, **the freelancer's invoice is exempt under art. 44 §1** — no VAT flows at all. This is the cleaner arrangement but requires the praktijk to take formal ownership of the patient relationship.

**Cost-sharing association / zelfstandige groepering (art. 44 §1bis WBTW)**

A group of self-employed logopedists can set up a formal "zelfstandige groepering van personen" (independent group of persons) that charges back to its members the exact cost of shared services (reception, software, cleaning, utilities) **VAT-exempt**, provided very strict conditions are met:

1. All members exercise an activity that is VAT-exempt under art. 44 (therapy only);
2. The groepering charges members only the exact reimbursement of costs (no profit markup);
3. The exemption does not distort competition (CJEU case C-274/15 Commission v. Luxembourg and subsequent case law have sharply narrowed this);
4. The groepering is formally constituted (typically as a maatschap, VZW, or rarely a CV);
5. Prior notification to the FOD Financiën (form 604A with specific indication).

In practice this is underused because (a) the "no distortion of competition" condition is interpreted strictly and (b) if any member does any VAT-taxable activity, the exemption is broken. For a "pure therapy" cost-sharing maatschap it remains a viable construction and is sometimes recommended by tax advisors.

### 3.5 VAT on travel / kilometer invoicing

When a logopedist travels to the patient's home and charges a **huisbezoek supplement** (see section 5), the supplement follows the character of the underlying service:

- If the underlying home visit is a **therapeutic act exempt under art. 44 §1**, the travel supplement is **also exempt** — it is an ancillary element of the exempt service ("accessoir volgt het principale").
- If the travel is charged as part of a **non-therapeutic service** (e.g. going to a company site to give a voice-coaching session), the travel is **taxable at the same rate as the main service** (21%).

When a praktijk **reimburses** a freelance logopedist for kilometers driven (see section 5), this is not a "sale" of travel — it is an internal cost reimbursement. If the freelancer invoices the praktijk for their services (VAT-exempt or 21% depending on direction), the kilometer reimbursement is either (a) embedded in the service fee and follows its VAT treatment, or (b) shown as a separate "kosten eigen aan de werkgever / opdrachtgever" line that is not subject to VAT because it is a pure reimbursement.

## 4. Income-tax specifics for logopedists

### 4.1 Deductible business expenses

The list of expenses most commonly deducted by self-employed Belgian logopedists and/or their praktijk BV:

**Premises and office**

- Rent for the praktijk room (professional lease) — 100% deductible;
- Utilities, cleaning, maintenance — 100% deductible;
- Insurance of the building — 100% deductible;
- For an eenmanszaak with a home office: professional portion of home rent/mortgage interest, utilities, household insurance, maintenance, prorated by the m² used for the praktijk (typical: 10%–25%).

**Home office: forfait vs. real**

If the logopedist works partially from home, the deduction is calculated on a **prorata of the home's area** used exclusively professionally. There is no fixed forfait for home office costs at the self-employed level (the 10% / 15% / 30% splits sometimes cited are actually rules for specific categories of costs within the real-expenses method, not a formal forfait). The employee "bureauvergoeding" regime (section 5.4) does NOT apply to self-employed persons.

**Software and subscriptions**

- halingo.be subscription — 100% deductible;
- MyCareNet / eAttest subscriptions (if any) — 100%;
- Electronic patient record software — 100%;
- Office suite (Microsoft 365, Google Workspace) — 100% if used professionally;
- Test material (databases of normed tests, digital assessment tools) — 100%;
- Online course subscriptions for continuing education — 100%.

**Therapy materials**

- Books, test kits, therapy cards, games, stimulus sets — 100% deductible;
- If depreciated: items under a certain threshold (typically €1,000) can be expensed directly; above that, depreciated over 3–5 years.

**Car and mobility — the tightening Belgian car regime**

Belgium has implemented one of the strictest car-deduction regimes in Europe, progressively phasing out fossil fuels. Current rules:

| Category | Rule 2025 | Rule 2026 | Rule 2027 | Rule 2028+ |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Fossil car purchased BEFORE 1 July 2023 | Gram formula (40%–100% based on CO₂ & fuel) | Unchanged, old regime grandfathered | Unchanged | Unchanged |
| Fossil car (new purchase/lease/rental) 1 July 2023 – 31 December 2025 | Gram formula capped at 75% max | **Capped at 50%** | **Capped at 25%** | **0% (fully phased out)** |
| Fossil car purchased from 1 January 2026 onward | 0% deductible | 0% | 0% | 0% |
| Plug-in hybrid (PHEV) purchased 2023–2025 | Gram formula, with fuel deductibility capped at 50% | 50% max | 25% | 0% |
| Plug-in hybrid purchased from 1 January 2026 (CO₂ < 75 g/km) | — | 120% – (0.5 × coefficient × g CO₂/km), BUT only for eenmanszaken; corporations 0% | tapers | 0% |
| 100% electric / hydrogen purchased in 2025 | **100%** | 100% | 100% | 100% (future reductions announced) |
| 100% electric purchased from 1 Jan 2027 | — | — | ~95% | declining schedule, down to 67.5% in 2031 |

**Gram formula (legacy)** — applied as: deduction % = 120% – (0.5 × coefficient × g CO₂/km), with coefficient 1 for diesel, 0.95 for petrol/CNG, 0.90 for CNG < 12 cv. Result floored at 50% and capped at 100% in the old regime, floored at 40% and capped at 100% in the intermediate regime.

The practical impact for logopedists who do a lot of home visits (thuiswerkbegeleiding, schoolbezoeken, institutional rounds): from 2026 onward, **only an EV or a PHEV (under conditions) offers any meaningful deduction**. Fossil cars for a praktijk BV bought from 1 January 2026 are **0% deductible**. Self-employed eenmanszaken benefit from a narrow PHEV exception until 2027.

**Professional liability insurance** — 100% deductible.

**Mutuality contributions** — the mutuality membership fee is NOT a professional expense (it's a personal health insurance). Only the professional portion of the small-risk supplement is sometimes deductible; in practice almost nothing is deducted here because social contributions already cover it.

**Sociale bijdragen** (quarterly RSVZ-paid contributions) — 100% deductible.

**VAPZ / PLCI** — 100% deductible, see section 1.1.

**IPT / EIP** for director of BV — The "Individuele Pensioentoezegging" (engagement individuel de pension) is an employer-funded supplementary pension for the BV's director; subject to the **80% rule** (total pension must not exceed 80% of last-year reference salary × career years / 40), the premium is deductible at the BV level. A logopedist with a praktijk BV can combine VAPZ at the personal level with IPT at the BV level — a stacked optimisation.

**Training and CPD (continuing professional development)**

- Courses, conferences, accredited trainings for the RIZIV peer-review / accreditation — 100% deductible;
- Professional books and journals — 100%.

**Representation expenses**

- Meals with professional contacts — **50% deductible** (art. 53 9° WIB92);
- Gifts and reception costs — **50% deductible**.

**Restaurant expenses** — 69% deductible (art. 53 8°bis WIB92, unchanged since 2005).

**Phone and internet**

- Professional portion of mobile phone and home internet — 75% deductible as a rule of thumb; must be justified; full deduction if exclusively professional.

**Depreciation of equipment**

- Therapy equipment, computers, tablets — 3 to 5 years linear depreciation;
- Furniture — 10 years linear;
- Building works / major renovations — 20 to 33 years.

### 4.2 Forfait vs. real expenses

For a typical full-time logopedist with a gross baten between €50,000 and €90,000, the forfaitaire aftrek (max €5,050 in 2025 for vrije beroepen) is almost always lower than real expenses:

| Expense category | Typical annual amount |
|---|---|
| Rent or home office | €4,000 – €9,000 |
| Software subscriptions | €500 – €1,500 |
| Therapy materials & books | €500 – €2,000 |
| Car (depreciation + fuel + insurance, after aftrekbeperking) | €2,000 – €6,000 |
| Professional insurance | €200 – €600 |
| CPD and conferences | €500 – €2,000 |
| VAPZ | €3,000 – €4,250 |
| Sociale bijdragen | €3,500 – €10,000 |
| Accountant | €1,000 – €3,000 |
| **Typical real total** | **€15,000 – €38,000** |

In almost all mature praktijken, **real expenses dominate** and the forfait is rejected. The forfait remains useful for a first partial year when receipts are scarce.

### 4.3 Income from mutualities, patients, schools, VAPH — tax treatment and proof

From a **tax/income** standpoint, all these revenue streams are **baten** of the logopedist's vrij beroep — there is no differentiation in rate or category. What differs is the **proof trail** expected by the FOD Financiën in case of audit:

| Source | Proof expected |
|---|---|
| **Patient private payment** (remgeld + full fee for non-conventioned) | Cash receipt or electronic payment log + copy of getuigschrift van verstrekte hulp / eAttest |
| **Mutuality direct payment (tiers payant via eAttest)** | MyCareNet reconciliation report showing paid transactions |
| **School, CLB, CPMS fees** | Service invoice to the school / local authority |
| **VAPH-funded therapy** (persoonsvolgend budget, RTH) | Contract with the VAPH-user, invoice to the user's budget holder (often the gezinsbudgethouder), VAPH-specific attestations |
| **Hospital or rehabilitation centre fees** (when employed or sub-contracted) | Invoices from the institution, payroll slips if employed |
| **Insurance reimbursement (private)** | Invoices with full patient reference |
| **Mutuality supplement (see section 6)** | Usually patient pays the logopedist in full, then submits to mutuality for refund — no direct impact on the logopedist's accounting |

**From a VAT standpoint**, all revenue above is covered by the art. 44 §1 exemption as long as it is therapeutic — no VAT applies regardless of the payer's identity. Only sales of goods (section 3.3) and non-therapeutic coaching/expertise break the exemption.

## 5. Kilometervergoeding and other allowances

### 5.1 The federal rate and its use as a ceiling

The Belgian federal civil servant kilometer allowance — set by **KB of 13 July 2017** regulating the reimbursement of travel expenses for federal civil servants — is the benchmark rate used across the economy. Since **1 October 2022**, the rate is **re-indexed quarterly** (previously annually on 1 July) by **Omzendbrief nr. 712** and its successors, based on an indexing mechanism combining fuel, maintenance and average-car-cost indicators.

**Rate history (EUR per kilometer, one-way, for professional trips using a private car)**:

| Period | Rate | Source |
|---|---|---|
| 1 July 2021 – 30 June 2022 | €0.3707 | KB 13 July 2017 |
| 1 July 2022 – 30 September 2022 | €0.4170 | Ad hoc indexation |
| 1 October 2022 – 31 December 2022 | €0.4201 | First quarterly indexation |
| 1 January 2023 – 31 March 2023 | €0.4259 | |
| Subsequent quarterly steps (rough range) | €0.41 – €0.44 | Quarterly index |
| 1 July 2024 – 30 September 2024 | ~€0.4293 | |
| 1 October 2024 – 31 December 2024 | ~€0.4297 | |
| 1 January 2025 – 31 March 2025 | ~€0.4315 (verify) | |
| **1 January 2026 – 31 March 2026** | **€0.4326 / km** | Circulaire 2026/C/13 (Forumforthefuture, Unisoc, Securex reports) |
| 1 April 2026 – 30 June 2026 | To be published mid-March 2026 | |

Halingo must load and update this rate quarterly, ideally fetching from the FOD BOSA / FOD Financiën circular or the Securex/Attentia public news feed.

**Ceiling function**

This federal rate is NOT a right for private-sector workers, but it functions as the **ceiling above which reimbursement becomes taxable salary**. Concretely:

- An employer can pay an employee up to €0.4326/km (Q1 2026) for business kilometers driven in a private car **tax-free and social-security-free** (treated as "kosten eigen aan de werkgever" — costs proper to the employer);
- Any amount paid above this ceiling is recharacterised as salary, subject to income tax withholding and social security contributions;
- The same ceiling applies between a principal and a self-employed service provider (e.g. praktijk BV reimbursing a freelance logopedist for home visits).

### 5.2 Employed vs self-employed treatment

**Employed logopedist (PC 330, or employed in a private praktijk)**

The employer reimburses the employee for home-visit travel at the federal rate (or a lower contractual rate). As long as the employer stays ≤ the federal ceiling, the reimbursement is social-security-free and income-tax-free. The employee must keep a **kilometer log** (date, origin, destination, distance, purpose) as proof — in case of audit, the RSZ/ONSS requires this. Some praktijken use flat monthly forfaits that must still be substantiated by an expected average distance per month.

Parallel: commuting from home to the fixed workplace is separately reimbursed — not under the km vergoeding regime but under a fixed per-km or public-transport reimbursement, subject to a separate tax exemption cap under art. 38 §1 9° WIB92 (around €490/year for the basic commute exemption, plus additional exemptions for bike/public transport).

**Self-employed freelancer reimbursed by the praktijk**

When a praktijk contracts a self-employed logopedist to do home visits and reimburses them at the federal km rate, the reimbursement is treated as a professional expense of the praktijk AND as taxable income of the freelancer. The freelancer then deducts their real car costs on their own PB return. The federal km rate functions as a convenient "kostenforfait" that the tax authority presumes to correspond to real costs if not exceeded.

**Self-employed logopedist deducting car costs for their own activity**

A self-employed logopedist who drives their own car for home visits does NOT apply the federal km rate directly — they deduct their **real car costs** (depreciation, fuel, insurance, maintenance, repairs, taxes) **subject to the aftrekbeperking** (see section 4.1 — the gram-formula or 0% phase-out for fossil cars from 2026). Alternatively, they can opt for the **forfait van €0.15/km for commuting** (art. 66bis WIB92) — but this forfait is only for the commute between home and the fixed workplace, not for business kilometers. For business kilometers, the real-cost method is the only option for a self-employed.

**Summary table**:

| Actor | Reimbursement method | Ceiling |
|---|---|---|
| Praktijk reimburses employed logopedist | Per-km at federal rate, tax-free if ≤ ceiling | €0.4326/km (Q1 2026) |
| Praktijk reimburses freelance logopedist | Per-km at federal rate, taxable income of freelancer; freelancer deducts own car costs on PB | No ceiling, but federal rate is "safe harbour" for the praktijk |
| Self-employed logopedist, own car, own business km | Real costs subject to aftrekbeperking (gram formula / 0% for fossil 2026+) | N/A — deduction formula applies |
| Employer reimburses commute home↔workplace | Fixed-commute reimbursement + exemption of ~€490/year | Art. 38 §1 9° WIB92 |

### 5.3 Home-visit billing to patients and RIZIV supplements

The RIZIV logopedie nomenclature (see document 01) contains specific codes for home-visit supplements — the logopedist can bill the patient an additional fee when therapy is delivered at the patient's residence, a school, or an institution, rather than at the praktijk.

**Home-visit nomenclature codes** (reference: RIZIV tariff document for logopedists, valid from 1 January 2026, published on riziv.fgov.be):

- Standard session codes (individual therapy, group therapy, evaluation) each have a corresponding **"huisbezoek" code** that bundles the session tariff with a travel supplement;
- Home visit supplement amounts are fixed quarterly in the RIZIV circular together with the session tariffs;
- The RIZIV tariffs for all logopedie services were indexed by **2.72% on 1 January 2026** per the convention 2026-2027.

Verify the exact codes and amounts in the current RIZIV tariff PDF (not reproduced in detail here — halingo.be should ingest this as a data file and update it at each tariff change). Document 01 of this knowledge base contains the full nomenclature table.

**Practical billing**: when the logopedist travels to the patient, they bill the home-visit code on the eAttest instead of the praktijk code. No additional "km fee" is invoiced to the patient — the patient is NOT charged a per-km rate. The home-visit supplement already includes an implicit travel compensation, set at a fixed amount regardless of actual distance. Some non-conventioned logopedists add a discretionary travel surcharge on top of the RIZIV honorarium; this is lawful but must be disclosed in advance (cost transparency per the communauteit-level patient-rights legislation — see document 02).

### 5.4 Maaltijdcheques, eco-cheques, bureauvergoeding, telework, GSM/internet

This section is about what the praktijk BV can offer to its **employees** (and, more restrictively, to its freelance contractors). The self-employed founder-logopedist cannot receive these advantages from their own eenmanszaak (an eenmanszaak cannot issue meal vouchers to itself), but they CAN receive them via their own BV — the BV is a legal employer and the founder-director is a bedrijfsleider.

**Maaltijdcheques / chèques-repas** (2025 → 2026)

Regulated by art. 19bis KB 28 November 1969 (social security) and art. 38 §1 25° WIB92 (income tax). To be tax- and social-security-exempt, the voucher must satisfy:

1. Employer contribution ≤ maximum; employee contribution ≥ minimum;
2. Granted per effective working day;
3. Not substituted for salary.

**Values effective from 1 January 2026** (per Salary Solution, Accofisc, Pluxee updates):

| Element | 2025 (historical) | **From 1 January 2026** |
|---|---|---|
| Maximum face value | €8.00 | **€10.00** |
| Maximum employer contribution | €6.91 | **€8.91** |
| Minimum employee contribution | €1.09 | €1.09 |
| Employer tax deduction (VenB) | €2.00 | **€4.00** (if max employer contribution granted, otherwise €2) |

**Eco-cheques / éco-chèques**

Regulated by CAO n° 98 of the Nationale Arbeidsraad. Maximum annual amount exempt from social security and income tax: **€250 per employee per year**. Eco-cheques are NOT deductible for the employer as a professional expense (art. 53 14° WIB92 excludes them), but they save the much larger employer social contribution + income tax that would apply if paid as salary.

**Representatiekosten forfait / frais de représentation**

The BV can grant the director a monthly forfait for representation expenses (meals, reception, client entertainment), tax-free at the director level and deductible at the BV level (within the 50% restaurant/representation limit). The FOD Financiën accepts forfaits up to roughly €250/month without specific justification for most bedrijfsleiders, more if the activity requires more representation. There is no formal legal cap but amounts above €250/month draw audit attention.

**Telewerkvergoeding / indemnité de télétravail — bureauvergoeding**

Under **Circulaire 2021/C/20 of 26 February 2021** (FOD Financiën), as updated in February 2025, an employer (including a praktijk BV paying its director) can grant a tax-free and social-security-free telework allowance ("bureauvergoeding") to employees who structurally work from home (≥ 1 day/week on average). The amount is capped at:

- **€154.74/month from 1 December 2023**;
- **€157.83/month from 1 March 2025** (indexed, per Securex, Attentia);
- Further indexations quarterly — verify via Securex lex4you.

The bureauvergoeding is intended to cover office costs (heating, electricity, coffee, small office supplies). It is cumulable with:

- Up to **€20/month** for the professional use of the employee's private internet;
- Up to **€20/month** for the professional use of the employee's private computer (alternatively, furnishing the computer by the employer exempts both parties).

For a praktijk BV, these are clean, well-documented tax-free top-ups to the director's remuneration — each one increases the director's net take-home by 100% of the amount (compared to ~45% for additional gross salary). Halingo.be should surface these as configurable on the director profile.

**GSM / mobile phone**

Providing a company mobile phone + subscription to the director triggers a **voordeel alle aard** (benefit in kind) of €72/year (or €36 if only the device is provided, or €36 if only the subscription is provided) — forfaited by FOD Financiën. This is highly efficient because the real cost to the BV is often €400–€600/year and the VAA booked on the director's side is only €72.

**PC / tablet / smartphone as tool**

A company laptop or tablet given for professional use triggers a VAA of **€72/year** per device, similarly. Internet at the director's home triggers a VAA of **€60/year**.

**Car as benefit in kind — voordeel alle aard**

The formula: VAA car = cataloguswaarde × 6/7 × CO₂ coefficient × age correction. The CO₂ coefficient is recalculated annually. A typical modern diesel mid-range car gives a VAA of €1,800–€2,400/year. Electric cars get the minimum coefficient (4%), bringing the VAA to a much lower floor — around €1,600/year minimum (the "minimum VAA car" is set by RB annually, ~€1,600 in 2025, verify).

## 6. Mutualities and supplementary insurance

### 6.1 The major mutualities and their logopedie coverage

In Belgium, mutualities ("ziekenfondsen" / "mutualités") administer the compulsory health insurance on behalf of the RIZIV/INAMI AND provide **supplementary voluntary insurance products** to their members. For logopedie, the **federally fixed reimbursement (via the RIZIV nomenclature)** is identical across all mutualities — there is no difference between CM and Solidaris at the federal/compulsory level. Differences appear at the **supplementary level**:

- Extra sessions beyond the RIZIV cap (for pathologies where the RIZIV imposes a session limit);
- Reimbursement for pathologies NOT covered by the RIZIV (e.g. mild articulation disorders below the RIZIV threshold);
- Subsidies for group therapy or specific conditions;
- Screenings for children.

**The five main mutuality families in Belgium** (2026 landscape):

1. **CM / MC — Christelijke Mutualiteit / Mutualité Chrétienne** — largest, Catholic-historical roots;
2. **Solidaris** — left/socialist-historical roots, rebranded from "Socialistische Mutualiteit" in 2022;
3. **Liberale Mutualiteit / Mutualité Libérale (LM)** — liberal-historical roots;
4. **Neutraal Ziekenfonds / Mutualité Neutre** — apolitical/neutral, includes Vlaams & Neutraal Ziekenfonds (VNZ);
5. **Onafhankelijke Ziekenfondsen / Mutualités Libres** — federation of independents (Partenamut, Helan, Freie Krankenkasse, OZ — now merged under Helan and other brands);
6. **HZIV / CAAMI** — Hulpkas voor Ziekte- en Invaliditeitsverzekering / Caisse Auxiliaire d'Assurance Maladie-Invalidité, the public federal auxiliary fund for people without mutuality affiliation.

**Supplementary logopedie benefits — typical 2025/2026 landscape** (amounts per session, as advertised by the mutualities in 2025):

| Mutuality | Supplementary benefit for logopedie | Source / condition |
|---|---|---|
| **CM / MC** | **€10/session** (**€15** with verhoogde tegemoetkoming / BIM), max 40 sessions ≤18 yr + 40 extra if on a rehabilitation centre waiting list; max 10 sessions ≥19 yr | cm.be/en/services-and-benefits/speech-therapy |
| **Solidaris Vlaanderen / Brabant** | €10/session, typically max 75 sessions/year for 2 years, or 150 sessions total (regional variation) | solidaris-vlaanderen.be, solidaris-brabant.be |
| **Liberale Mutualiteit (LM / ML)** | €10/session, max 100 sessions (or €1,000) per 3 calendar years | lm-ml.be |
| **Vlaams & Neutraal Ziekenfonds (VNZ)** | €10/session, limits vary | vnz.be |
| **Neutraal Ziekenfonds Vlaanderen** | €10/session; children-specific tussenkomsten in youth package | nzvl.be |
| **Helan (Onafhankelijke Ziekenfondsen)** | Tussenkomst per session; via "Helan+" supplementary plan | helan.be |
| **Partenamut** | Reimbursement under "Medicalia" supplementary insurance: **75% of fee, max €600/year** | partenamut.be |
| **HZIV / CAAMI** | Only compulsory insurance, no supplementary voordelen | caami-hziv.fgov.be |

**Key conditions** (common across most mutualities):

- Affiliation and up-to-date payment of the supplementary contribution (usually ~€8–€11/month);
- Prior written prescription by a physician (often specialist);
- The pathology is NOT already fully covered by the compulsory insurance (supplementary benefits are residual); OR the compulsory quota is exhausted; OR the pathology doesn't meet the RIZIV thresholds;
- A ceiling on total sessions reimbursed;
- Sometimes prior agreement by the mutuality's adviserend arts / médecin-conseil.

**How the SaaS should model this**:

- Per-patient, store the mutuality affiliation and the status of supplementary coverage;
- Per session, flag whether the patient is using the federal nomenclature reimbursement or the supplementary plan;
- Provide a "mutuality reminder" feature that warns the therapist when a patient is approaching their annual/total supplementary cap;
- For patient-facing invoicing, generate a "to be submitted to mutuality" attestation where needed (paper or PDF), because supplementary benefits typically don't flow through MyCareNet — they are paid reimbursed ex post by the mutuality to the patient.

### 6.2 Private hospitalisatie insurance coverage of logopedie

Several private insurers offer **ambulante zorg** (ambulatory care) supplements on top of their hospitalisatie-verzekering products. These reimburse non-hospital medical costs including logopedie under certain conditions.

| Insurer | Product line | Logopedie coverage |
|---|---|---|
| **DKV Belgium** | "DKV Daily" plans (Daily Essential, Daily Premium, Daily +) | Reimbursement of ambulante zorg kosten including logopedie, subject to **prior approval by the adviserend arts** confirming medical necessity; capped to an annual amount per product |
| **AG Insurance** | "Medi-Assistance" / "Hospi Flex" with ambulante optie | Logopedie included in paramedische sublimiet, prior approval often required |
| **Ethias** | Ambulante-zorg module | Variable by plan |
| **Allianz** | Hospi + ambulant | Variable |
| **Baloise / Athora** | Hospi + ambulant | Variable |
| **Partena Ziekenfonds (supplementary insurance) Hospitalia** | Hospitalia Plus includes some ambulant coverage | Limited |

**Key points**:

- Logopedie is usually reimbursed only for pathologies the insurer recognises as medically necessary (dysphasia, aphasia, dyslexia, post-stroke rehabilitation, etc.);
- "Developmental delay" without a clear medical diagnosis is often rejected;
- Pre-existing conditions clauses may exclude logopedie for children diagnosed before the policy start;
- The reimbursement is typically **residual**: compulsory insurance + mutuality supplementary + remgeld, and the private insurance covers part of the patient's out-of-pocket;
- Patient submits paper invoices to the insurer after the session.

**SaaS implication**: these flows do not integrate with MyCareNet; they are fully manual. The SaaS should at minimum produce a **detailed patient invoice** (suitable for the patient to submit to their insurer), including diagnosis code, session date, therapist RIZIV number, amount, RIZIV reimbursement deducted, and remaining remgeld.

### 6.3 Patient-side tax deductibility of logopedie

Under the Belgian personal income tax code, **medical and paramedical fees paid by a patient are in principle NOT deductible** for ordinary taxpayers — they are considered normal private living expenses. Exceptions:

1. **Buitengewone kosten for persons with a handicap or for a disabled child** — the "extra costs" related to the disability can be deducted under specific conditions (art. 104 WIB92, now largely integrated in the forfait voor zware handicap / aanvullende tegemoetkoming). Logopedie expenses for a child with a recognised handicap can be included here, subject to documentation.
2. **Onderhoudsgelden** — not directly applicable.
3. **Kinderoppas-aftrek** — for children under 14, some care costs are deductible, but logopedie is not in the list (it's medical care, not childcare).

In general, halingo should NOT promote any "tax deductibility" angle for patients on its billing pages — the deduction is the exception, not the rule, and misleading claims could trigger complaints.

## 7. Bookkeeping and accounting obligations

### 7.1 Per legal form

**Natural-person self-employed (eenmanszaak)**

Regulated by art. III.82 et seq. Wetboek Economisch Recht (Book III, Titel 3 — Boekhoudkundige Verplichtingen). A self-employed vrij beroep (logopedist) may keep a **vereenvoudigde boekhouding** (single-entry accounting) as long as annual turnover (excl. VAT) does not exceed **€500,000** (art. III.85 WER). This threshold was originally set by the 17 July 1975 accounting law and codified in the WER.

The vereenvoudigde boekhouding consists of:

- **Financial journal** (aankoopboek / inkoopboek — purchases, verkoopboek — sales, financieel boek — bank and cash movements);
- **Inventaris** at year-end;
- No balance sheet or annual accounts filed with the NBB.

**Above €500,000**: mandatory double-entry accounting ("dubbele boekhouding"), annual accounts (though without NBB filing obligation for a natural person).

**BV (vennootschap)**

Regardless of size, a BV must keep **double-entry accounting** and file **annual accounts** with the **Nationale Bank van België (NBB)** under art. 3:10 et seq. WVV. The annual accounts consist of balance sheet, income statement, notes, and (for non-micro) social balance sheet.

Filing deadline: **30 days after the general assembly** approving the accounts, and no later than **7 months after the end of the financial year** (art. 3:10 WVV). Late filing triggers the "bijdrage kosten publicatie" surcharge and ultimately fines.

**Micro-company status** (art. 1:25 WVV): if the BV does not exceed more than one of (a) balance sheet total €450,000, (b) turnover €900,000, (c) 10 FTE average, it qualifies as a micro-vennootschap and may file a **simplified annual accounts schema** (micro-schema). Most solo-praktijk BVs qualify as micro.

**Small company** (art. 1:24 WVV): the broader "klein" category (see section 2.1 thresholds) is used for the reduced corporate tax rate and simpler audit obligations; not to be confused with the "micro" accounting simplification.

### 7.2 Invoicing rules — health care specifics

**Mandatory invoice fields** under art. 5 KB n° 1 WBTW (for VAT-taxable invoices):

1. Date of the invoice and sequential invoice number;
2. Name, address, BTW number of the supplier (the praktijk or the therapist);
3. Name, address, and, if taxable, BTW number of the customer;
4. Date of the operation (if different from invoice date);
5. Description of the goods/services with sufficient detail;
6. Unit prices, quantities, total price (excl. VAT);
7. Applicable VAT rate and VAT amount;
8. Reference to the exemption if applicable (e.g. "Vrijgesteld van BTW overeenkomstig art. 44 §1 WBTW");
9. Total invoice amount including VAT.

For **exempt medical care**, an invoice in the formal VAT sense is NOT mandatory because the operation is out of scope of the VAT invoicing regime. Instead, logopedists use the **getuigschrift voor verstrekte hulp** (care certificate, a RIZIV-standardised form) as evidence of care delivered, OR the digital **eAttest** via MyCareNet (see document 03).

**Patient receipt vs. invoice vs. eAttest**:

- **eAttest** — digital attestation sent directly to the patient's mutuality via MyCareNet; the patient does not need a paper receipt for reimbursement, everything is automatic. The eAttest is NOT a VAT invoice — it is a proof of care-supply in the sense of the sickness-insurance regulation.
- **Getuigschrift voor verstrekte hulp** — the legacy paper version of the eAttest, still in use for some situations.
- **Sales invoice** for ancillary goods (books, materials) — a normal VAT invoice is required (or a receipt, depending on the amount and the purchaser's status).
- **Service invoice** for non-therapeutic services (coaching, expertise) — normal VAT invoice, with 21% (or exemption code if under kleine-onderneming regeling).

**For freelance retrocessions**: each monthly retrocession invoice between a praktijk BV and a freelance logopedist is a normal business-to-business invoice, subject to standard invoicing rules. If retrocession is labelled as room rental + admin services, the invoice is taxable (21%), unless a cost-sharing arrangement is formally in place.

### 7.3 Retention of accounting records

**The 2022/2023 reform**: the **wet van 20 november 2022** (Official Gazette 30 November 2022) amended art. 354 WIB92 and art. 315 WIB92 to extend the tax assessment period and, consequentially, the record retention period from **7 years to 10 years** for all tax and accounting documents relevant to income tax.

**Important nuance**:

- **Accountancy law** (Book III WER) still formally requires a **7-year** retention period for accounting books and supporting documents (art. III.86 WER);
- **Tax law** (WIB92) now requires **10 years** for the tax authority's audit rights, and thus effectively requires the same 10-year retention for source documents relevant to the tax return;
- The practical rule is: **keep everything 10 years**.

**Starting point**: the 10-year period starts on **1 January of the year following the financial year**. For documents relating to income year 2022 (= AJ 2023), the retention runs until **31 December 2032**.

**Exceptions**:

- **Real estate**: documents related to immovable property must be kept **15 years** (or 25 years in some cases), because the VAT adjustment period for construction is 15 years.
- **UBO declarations**: no specific retention because the UBO register itself is maintained by the state.

**Electronic retention**: art. III.89 §1 WER and the KB of 12 September 2013 allow electronic storage provided the documents are readable and cannot be modified; halingo.be's archive must therefore guarantee integrity (audit trail, hash, timestamp).

### 7.4 UBO register

The **UBO register** (Ultimate Beneficial Owner register / register van de uiteindelijke begunstigden) is administered by the Algemene Administratie van de Thesaurie at FOD Financiën. It was created by the **wet van 18 september 2017** (anti-money-laundering law, transposing AMLD4) and detailed by the **KB of 30 July 2018**. Further reforms by the **KB of 23 September 2020** tightened the disclosure of supporting documents.

**Obligation**: every Belgian legal entity (BV, NV, CV, VZW, maatschap, etc.) must register its ultimate beneficial owners in the UBO register and **annually reconfirm** the data (even if unchanged). Changes must be reported **within 30 days**.

**Who is a UBO**: any natural person who directly or indirectly owns more than **25% of shares, voting rights, or control**, or who otherwise exercises control over the entity. For a solo-praktijk BV owned 100% by the logopedist, the logopedist is the sole UBO.

**Sanctions**: administrative fines from **€250 to €50,000** (art. 132 AML law). In February–March 2024 the Belgian State Gazette reported the **removal of nearly 21,000 entities from the KBO/BCE** for persistent non-compliance with UBO obligations (per Freshfields client alert, Lexgo).

**Halingo implication**: the SaaS should remind praktijk owners annually to reconfirm the UBO register (perhaps around the anniversary of incorporation), and the onboarding checklist should include a "UBO registered?" question.

## 8. SaaS implications — what halingo.be must support

Given everything above, the top 10 things halingo.be's billing/accounting/pay-out module must get right:

1. **Per-therapist VAT regime** — each therapist profile has one of: (a) fully exempt art. 44 §1 (no BTW number), (b) mixed with vrijstellingsregeling kleine onderneming, (c) gemengde belastingplichtige with real VAT (real 21% charged on non-therapeutic revenue; prorata or actual-use deduction). Rate updates must be loaded from a maintained table.

6. **RIZIV tariff and home-visit supplement table** — the SaaS must ingest the RIZIV logopedist tariff (currently valid from 1 January 2026 with 2.72% indexation) and automatically bill the correct code + supplement when a session is logged as home/school/institution visit. 7. **Mutuality supplementary benefit tracking** — per-patient visibility of the current state of the mutuality's supplementary package (annual/total sessions cap, residual). 8. **Multi-entity organisation model** — a praktijk organisation can have one or more BVs (op BV + management BV), multiple therapist profiles each with their own status (employed PC 330 / freelance / director), their own VAT regime, and their own sub-accounts. The roll-up for director dashboards must aggregate across all entities while keeping per-entity ledgers clean.

9. **Expense and allowance tracking per therapist** — maaltijdcheques, ecocheques, bureauvergoeding (€157.83/month 2025+), GSM/internet VAA, car VAA, telework days, restaurant receipts (69% deductible), representation expenses (50%), VAPZ contributions. The SaaS should integrate with or export to standard Belgian payroll software (SD Worx, Acerta, Securex, Partena, Liantis) via their API or file formats.

A "compliance radar" that turns these into actionable alerts is a high-value product hook.

**Bonus** (not in the top 10 but important): the system must handle the **10-year record retention** in an integrity-preserving way (WORM storage, audit trails, export-on-demand) to meet art. 315/354 WIB92 and art. III.86 WER requirements.
