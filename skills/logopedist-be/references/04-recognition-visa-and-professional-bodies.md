# 04 — Recognition, Visa, Diploma, and Professional Bodies for Belgian Logopedists

> Last verified: 2026-04-06
> Scope: How one becomes and remains a recognized logopedist in Belgium, and how halingo.be can verify a user is a legitimate practitioner.

This document maps the complete legal and administrative pathway from "graduate of a logopedie programme" to "fully practising, billable logopedist in Belgium". It is opinionated about what halingo needs to know to (a) verify a user, (b) bill correctly, and (c) keep its practitioners compliant.

---

## 1. Legal foundation — who is a logopedist under Belgian law

The right to use the protected professional title "logopedist" (NL) / "logopède" (FR) / "Logopäde" (DE) is anchored in federal law and a layer of royal decrees that pre-date the 2014 transfer of recognition powers to the Communities.

### 1.1 The umbrella law — Gecoördineerde wet 10 mei 2015 / Loi coordonnée 10 mai 2015

Logopedie is a **paramedisch beroep / profession paramédicale** as defined in the **Wet betreffende de uitoefening van de gezondheidszorgberoepen, gecoördineerd op 10 mei 2015** ("WUG" / "Loi LEPSS"), published in the Belgisch Staatsblad on 18 June 2015. The 2015 coordinated law is the consolidated successor to **Koninklijk Besluit nr. 78 van 10 november 1967 betreffende de uitoefening van de gezondheidszorgberoepen** (the famous "KB nr. 78") and its old Article 22bis on paramedical professions. References in older literature to "Article 22bis KB 78" should be read today as the corresponding articles in the 2015 coordinated law.

The coordinated law:

- defines who may exercise a healthcare profession in Belgium;
- requires every practitioner to hold a federal **visum** issued by the FOD Volksgezondheid / SPF Santé publique;
- makes practising without a visum a criminal offence (illegal exercise of a healthcare profession);
- delegates the determination of qualification requirements and the list of acts to royal decree (KB / AR).

### 1.2 The profession-specific decree — KB 20 oktober 1994 / AR 20 octobre 1994

The defining instrument for the profession is the **Koninklijk Besluit van 20 oktober 1994 betreffende de beroepstitel en de kwalificatievereisten voor de uitoefening van het beroep van logopedist en houdende vaststelling van de lijst van de technische prestaties en van de lijst van de handelingen waarmee de logopedist door een arts kan worden belast** (Justel/eJustice ID 1994102052; published in the Belgisch Staatsblad / Moniteur belge on 6 December 1994). This is the single most important legal text for halingo to know about — almost every page on FOD Volksgezondheid, Departement Zorg, and the Communities references it.

KB 20.10.1994 does three things:

1. Reserves the **professional title** "logopedist" / "logopède" to people who satisfy the qualification requirements in Article 3.
2. Sets the **diploma and internship requirements** (see §2 below): minimum 3 years of full-time higher education (≥ 180 ECTS), a curriculum that covers a list of theoretical and practical subjects, ≥ 600 supervised internship hours across multiple sectors and pathologies, and a final dissertation.
3. Establishes the **scope of practice** through two annexes: Annex I lists the **technische prestaties** (autonomous "technical performances": logopedic examination, written technical reports, treatment of language/speech/voice/hearing/swallowing disorders, etc.) and Annex II lists the **toevertrouwde handelingen** (acts a physician may delegate: prevention and detection of communication disorders, the technical component of audiometry, vestibulometry, phonetography).

KB 20.10.1994 has been amended several times — notably by the **KB van 18 november 2004** (which tightened the recognition regime and the relationship with grandfather practitioners) and by adjustments via the **KB van 2 juli 2009** (paramedical recognition framework). The April 2014 transposition cut-off (see §3.1) is also rooted in these amendments.

### 1.3 What a logopedist may and may not do

Read literally, the technical performances in Annex I are autonomous: a logopedist is not a delegated assistant of a physician for the listed acts. However, **billing of those acts under the verplichte verzekering** (RIZIV/INAMI) is gated by a separate regime — the patient must hold a medical prescription and a treatment authorisation by the medisch adviseur of the mutualiteit, governed by Article 36 of the **nomenclatuur van de geneeskundige verstrekkingen** (see the sibling document `01-riziv-nomenclature-and-tariffs.md`). Recognition gets you the right to *practise*; recognition + RIZIV registration + a valid prescription + an Article 36 indication get you the right to *bill*.

## 2. Required diploma pathways

KB 20.10.1994 sets the federal minimum, but the actual diplomas are issued by the Communities, which have full competence over higher education. Two academically distinct routes coexist in Belgium today, plus historical "afwijkings"/"dérogation" pathways.

### 2.1 Flemish-Community pathway (NL)

In Flanders, two diplomas grant access to the title:

- **Professionele bachelor "logopedie en audiologie"** (180 ECTS, 3 years), offered by the hogescholen — Arteveldehogeschool Gent, HOGENT, Thomas More Antwerpen, VIVES, AP Hogeschool, etc. The first two years are common to logopedie and audiologie; in year 3 students choose a specialisation. This is the most common route into the profession. It maps directly onto the federal minimum of 180 ECTS / 3 years / 600 internship hours required by KB 20.10.1994.
- **Master of Science in de logopedische en audiologische wetenschappen** (academic, 180 ECTS bachelor + 120 ECTS master = 5 years), offered at KU Leuven and Universiteit Gent. Within the master, students pick a logopedie or audiologie afstudeerrichting. This path is required for clinical-research positions (e.g. university hospitals, COS centres) but is *not* legally required to obtain the federal recognition, the visum, or a RIZIV number — the professional bachelor is sufficient.

Both diplomas are explicitly listed by the Departement Zorg as automatically triggering recognition.

### 2.2 French-Community pathway (FR)

In Wallonia and Brussels (FR), the academic route dominates:

- **Bachelier (transition) en logopédie** (180 ECTS, 3 years) followed by a **Master en logopédie** (120 ECTS, 2 years) — total 300 ECTS over 5 years. The master is co-organised by ULB and UCLouvain in Brussels, and ULiège runs its own master at the Faculté de Psychologie. The bachelier is **not professionnalisant**; only the master diploma grants the title of logopède in the FWB system.
- A **bachelier professionnalisant en logopédie** (180 ECTS, 3 years) is also offered by Hautes Écoles (e.g. HE Vinci, HE Léonard de Vinci, HEPL). This is the structural equivalent of the Flemish professional bachelor and is sufficient for federal recognition.

In the FWB, a logopède with only the bachelier professionnalisant has the same federal/RIZIV status as a master-trained logopède — the master is required for some hospital ranks and academic careers, not for legal practice.

### 2.3 German-speaking Community

The Deutschsprachige Gemeinschaft has no full Logopädie programme of its own. German-speaking candidates typically study in the FWB (Liège/Louvain-la-Neuve) or in Germany; the German-speaking Community (Ministerium der DG) handles recognition for residents who graduated abroad. There is no German-language professional union in Belgium specific to logopedie of comparable scale to VVL/UPLF.

### 2.4 Internship and final-thesis requirements

Article 3 of KB 20.10.1994 imposes:

- **≥ 600 hours of supervised internship** spread across "verschillende activiteitssectoren en pathologieën" / "différents secteurs d'activité et pathologies" of human communication disorders, documented in a **stageboek / carnet de stage**;
- a **final dissertation / mémoire** related to the training and the internship;
- continuous knowledge maintenance via continuing education (see §11).

The Flemish professional bachelor typically delivers ~700–900 stage-uren; the FWB master delivers two stages of 300+325h or 300+400h. Both comfortably meet the federal minimum.

### 2.5 Historical / grandfather pathways ("afwijking" / "dérogation")

KB 20.10.1994 has, since its successive amendments, contained an **afwijkingsregime / régime de dérogation** for people who were practising logopedie before 6 December 1994 (the original publication date) and could prove ≥ 3 years of activity attested by a physician. The deadline to invoke that pathway expired long ago and provisional recognitions had to be converted to full recognition by **2 April 2019**. Halingo can in 2026 safely assume that **every active practitioner today holds either a recognised diploma or a long-finalised dérogation** — the grandfather window is closed.

## 3. FOD/SPF recognition (agrément / erkenning)

### 3.1 Who is competent — federal vs. Community

Originally the federal Minister of Public Health granted recognition for paramedical professions. The **Sixième Réforme de l'État / Zesde Staatshervorming** (Bijzondere wet 6 januari 2014) transferred this competence to the Communities **with effect from 1 January 2016** (de facto from 1 July 2014 for some files). Today:

| Region of residence | Competent recognition authority | Ministry / Department |
|---|---|---|
| Flanders | **Departement Zorg** (formerly Agentschap Zorg en Gezondheid) | Vlaamse overheid |
| Wallonia + Brussels (FR) | **Direction de l'Agrément des Prestataires de Soins de Santé (DAPSS)** | Fédération Wallonie-Bruxelles (`agrementsante.cfwb.be`) |
| German-speaking Community | **Ministerium der Deutschsprachigen Gemeinschaft** | DG Live (`ostbelgienlive.be`) |

The federal level (FOD Volksgezondheid / SPF Santé publique, Directoraat-generaal Gezondheidszorg) retains responsibility for issuing the **visum** that authorises the practitioner to exercise the healthcare profession on Belgian territory and for maintaining the federal cadastre **eCad**.

### 3.2 The Belgian-diploma route — automatic erkenning + automatic visum

For a logopedist who graduated from a recognised Belgian institution, the procedure is **fully automated** since the post-2014 reforms:

1. The hogeschool/universiteit transmits the diploma data to the competent Community via an electronic database (Higher Education Database / DHO in Flanders, equivalent in FWB).
2. The Community **automatically issues the recognition certificate** ("erkenningsattest" / "attestation d'agrément") and sends it by post (and via eBox) to the official address from the Rijksregister.
3. The Community **electronically notifies FOD Volksgezondheid**, which then **automatically issues the visum** and pushes it to the practitioner's eBox and the **Visumportaal** (`health.belgium.be/nl/tools/visumportaal-gezondheidszorg`).

There is **no application form, no fee, and no waiting queue** for the standard Belgian-diploma case. In practice the whole flow takes a few weeks after diploma proclamation. New graduates who have not received their attest after ~2 months should contact their Community recognition desk directly.

### 3.3 The non-automatic route

Practitioners with a foreign diploma (EU/EEA/Swiss → §5; non-EU → §6), or with a missing element in their file, must submit a manual recognition request to the competent Community:

- **Flanders**: via the Departement Zorg portal (`departementzorg.be`).
- **FWB**: via `agrementsante.cfwb.be` — submit a `Formulaire de demande de reconnaissance` plus annexes by email to `agrementsante@cfwb.be`.
- **DG**: via the Ministerium der DG.

Documents typically required: copy of identity document, diploma + diplomasupplement (with sworn translation if not in NL/FR/DE), course programme with hours/ECTS, internship logbook, mémoire/eindwerk excerpts, and CV. Once the file is complete, the Community has up to **4 months** to decide (FWB explicitly), with an **acknowledgement of receipt within 1 month**. After a positive decision, FOD Volksgezondheid issues the visum automatically.

### 3.4 The visum's legal weight

The visum is a federal authorisation to *practise* the profession. It is independent of any RIZIV registration or convention status (§4). A logopedist with a visum but no RIZIV number may legally treat patients but cannot deliver an attest of care that triggers RIZIV reimbursement.

A visum can be **suspended or withdrawn** by FOD Volksgezondheid in cases of physical/psychological unfitness, criminal conviction, or grave breach of professional duties (procedure under the coordinated law of 10 May 2015, including a Toezichtcommissie / Commission de contrôle).

## 4. RIZIV/INAMI visa and number

### 4.1 What the RIZIV number is — and what it is not

The **RIZIV-nummer / numéro INAMI** is an identifier issued by the **Rijksinstituut voor Ziekte- en Invaliditeitsverzekering / Institut National d'Assurance Maladie-Invalidité (RIZIV/INAMI)** to healthcare providers who want their acts to be reimbursable under the verplichte verzekering voor geneeskundige verzorging. It is **not** a licence to practise — that is the visum (§3). It is the key to the billing system: every getuigschrift voor verstrekte hulp / attestation de soins donnés that a logopedist hands to a patient must carry their RIZIV number.

### 4.2 Structure

The RIZIV number for an individual healthcare provider is **11 digits**, conceptually written as `Y/X/XXXX/CD/OOO`:

- a sequence identifying the practitioner;
- a check digit (CD, the 8th–9th positions);
- a **3-digit bevoegdheidscode / code de qualification** as the **last three digits** that identifies the profession and sub-specialty.

The official lists of bevoegdheidscodes are published per profession on `riziv.fgov.be/nl/professionals/info-voor-allen/bevoegdheidscodes` (and the parallel French page). For logopedisten the dedicated PDF (`bevoegdheidscodes_logopedisten.pdf`) lists the active codes; halingo should treat the RIZIV PDF as the source of truth and not hard-code values, because they have evolved with the convention regime. **Practical hint for verification:** the last three digits of any RIZIV number passed by a halingo user must match a valid logopedie bevoegdheidscode for that user to be allowed to bill logopedische verstrekkingen.

### 4.3 The application process

After the visum has been issued, the practitioner must actively **register with RIZIV**:

1. Download the form **"Aanvraag tot registratie als logopedist bij het RIZIV"** / **"Demande d'enregistrement comme logopède auprès de l'INAMI"** from `riziv.fgov.be/nl/professionals/individuele-zorgverleners/logopedisten/formulieren-voor-de-logopedisten`.
2. Complete and sign it; attach a copy of the visum and the recognition attest if requested.
3. Send it to RIZIV — by email (`logonl@riziv-inami.fgov.be` for NL files, `logofr@riziv-inami.fgov.be` for FR files) or to one of the regional desks (e.g. Guffenslaan 33, 3500 Hasselt for the Limburg desk).
4. RIZIV verifies recognition and visum data against the federal cadastre, then mails the **RIZIV number** to the official contact address. In practice this takes **2–4 weeks** once the file is complete.

The form simultaneously asks the practitioner whether they wish to **adhere to the national convention** (see §4.4). The new registrant has **up to 30 days after registration** to refuse adherence in writing (`U kunt tot 30 dagen na uw inschrijving weigeren om toe te treden tot de overeenkomst`).

### 4.4 Recognition vs convention status — a critical distinction

There are three legal statuses, and halingo must model them separately:

| Status | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Visum** (federal) | Legal authorisation to practise the profession of logopedist. Necessary to use the title. |
| **RIZIV-registratie** (numéro INAMI) | The practitioner is identified in the RIZIV billing system and can deliver reimbursable attests. |
| **Geconventioneerd / conventionné** (toetreding tot de overeenkomst) | The practitioner has signed up to the national tariefovereenkomst between the representative logopedie unions (VVL, UPLF, etc.) and the verzekeringsinstellingen (mutualiteiten) negotiated in the Overeenkomstencommissie at RIZIV. They charge official tariffs and the patient gets full terugbetaling; in exchange the practitioner is eligible for the RIZIV-toelage / sociaal statuut. |

Refusing to be conventioneerd does not affect the right to practise or the right to bill — the practitioner can deliver reimbursable attests but charges higher fees and the patient receives a **lower reimbursement** (the so-called **"–25% regel"** when ≥ 60% of the profession is in the convention; for the 2024–2025 convention 67.38% adhered, so the rule applied). Halingo's billing UI must be aware of the practitioner's convention status because it determines which honorarium values to inject into the getuigschrift.

### 4.5 The "RIZIV-toelage" / sociaal statuut

A geconventioneerd logopedist who meets activity thresholds is entitled to a **sociale toelage / avantage social** — for fiscal year 2026 between **€1,560.89 and €3,219.31** — which RIZIV pays directly into a supplementary pension contract (VAPZ-equivalent with social character). Eligibility, per the RIZIV sociaal-statuut page:

- full-year adhesion to the convention;
- a **minimum** of 900 prestations (or 15,750 R-waarden) for the lower toelage and 2,000 prestations / 35,000 R for the higher one;
- a **maximum ceiling** of 4,000 prestations / 70,000 R — above that the practitioner is presumed to have a too-large activity to be eligible for the sociaal voordeel.

Halingo's analytics layer can usefully surface a "running count of prestaties / R-values this calendar year" to help practitioners stay inside the eligibility band.

## 5. EU diploma recognition

EU/EEA/Swiss-trained logopedists are covered by **Directive 2005/36/EC** on the recognition of professional qualifications, transposed into Belgian federal law (with extensive Community-level transposition by the Vlaamse Gemeenschap, the FWB, and the Deutschsprachige Gemeinschaft).

### 5.1 Competent authority

Logopedie is **not in the list of automatic-recognition professions** in the directive (those are doctor, dentist, pharmacist, midwife, nurse, veterinarian, architect). It falls under the **general system** (Articles 10 et seq. of the directive), which means the host authority compares education and may impose compensation measures.

- **Flanders**: Departement Zorg (`departementzorg.be/nl/uw-erkenning-aanvragen-voor-een-gezondheidsberoep-op-basis-van-een-diploma-uit-de-eer-zwitserland`).
- **FWB**: DAPSS — `agrementsante.cfwb.be`, page "J'ai un diplôme délivré par un État membre de l'EEE → Profession paramédicale → Logopède".

### 5.2 Documents

A typical FWB file requires:

- demande form;
- ID;
- the diploma + supplement with **sworn French translation**;
- attestation of conformity per Article 11 of Directive 2005/36/EC issued by the home-state competent authority;
- official course programme (theoretical and practical hours / ECTS) with sworn translation;
- detailed internship description (objectives, hours, places, evaluations);
- mémoire excerpts;
- professional employment attestations (for any post-graduation experience);
- a **DALF C2 French-language certificate** (the FWB explicitly demands "connaissance approfondie", level C2; lower levels are not accepted).

Flanders applies an analogous regime in Dutch (and may require a NT2 / Inburgering attestation depending on the case).

### 5.3 Compensation measures and timelines

After comparing the candidate's training with the Belgian programme, the competent authority can:

1. grant recognition without restriction;
2. impose **compensation measures** under Article 14 of the directive — either an **épreuve d'aptitude** (aptitude test) or a **stage d'adaptation** of up to **3 years**, at the candidate's choice (a model `convention de stage` is provided by FWB);
3. refuse recognition.

The decision must be issued within **4 months from completeness** of the file, with an acknowledgement of receipt within **1 month**. After a positive decision, the practitioner submits the recognition to FOD Volksgezondheid, which issues the visum, and then files a RIZIV registration as in §4.

### 5.4 The European Professional Card (EPC)

The EPC is **not currently available for logopedists** — only nurses (general care), pharmacists, physiotherapists, mountain guides, and real-estate agents are covered. EU-trained logopedists must use the standard general-system procedure.

## 6. Non-EU diploma recognition

Non-EU candidates face a **two-step** procedure that is significantly more administrative:

1. **Academic equivalence** of the diploma at the appropriate Community body:
   - **NARIC-Vlaanderen** (`naricvlaanderen.be`) for Flanders;
   - **Service de la reconnaissance académique et professionnelle des diplômes étrangers d'enseignement supérieur (Equisup, `equisup.cfwb.be`)** for FWB;
   - the Ministerium der DG for the Deutschsprachige Gemeinschaft.

   NARIC/Equisup decides which Belgian diploma the foreign one is equivalent to, taking into account ECTS, internship hours, and curriculum content.

2. **Professional recognition** from the Community recognition desk (Departement Zorg / DAPSS / DG), based on the equivalence + the standard documentary file (ID, equivalence decision, programme, stages, language certificate, etc.). Compensation measures (aptitude test, adaptation stage) are commonly imposed.

After the Community's positive decision, the federal visum and the RIZIV number follow as in the EU case. Total elapsed time: **realistically 9–18 months**, sometimes more if compensation measures are required.

Some non-EU diplomas (e.g. from Canada, the United States, Australia) are highly regarded, but there is no fast-track. Candidates from francophone Africa or the Maghreb usually obtain equivalence faster than candidates from countries with very different curricula because Equisup has historical reference files.

## 7. Self-employed vs employed practice

Logopedie is typically a **vrij beroep / profession libérale**, but a non-trivial fraction works under an employment contract (CLB/CPMS, hospitals, rehabilitation centres, special schools, COS centres, etc.). The legal/billing setup differs.

### 7.1 Self-employed (zelfstandige / indépendant)

Required steps once the visum and RIZIV number are in place:

1. **KBO/BCE registration** — obtain an **ondernemingsnummer / numéro d'entreprise** via an erkend ondernemingsloket (Acerta, Liantis, Securex, Xerius, Partena…). Logopedie is registered under the appropriate NACEBEL code for paramedical activity.
2. **Sociaal verzekeringsfonds / caisse d'assurances sociales** — joining one is a legal obligation under the social statute of self-employed persons. It calculates quarterly social contributions on income.
3. **BTW / TVA** — logopedische verstrekkingen are **vrijgesteld van btw / exonérés de TVA** under Article 44, §1, 1° of the BTW-wetboek (medical care exemption); the practitioner still needs a btw-nummer (which equals the ondernemingsnummer prefixed by `BE`) but does not charge VAT on therapy.
4. **Mutualiteit** — notify the personal mutualiteit of the change of status.
5. **Bookkeeping** — single-entry bookkeeping is allowed; the practitioner may choose accrual accounting voluntarily.
6. **Insurances** — see §12.

### 7.2 Employed (loontrekkende / salarié)

Logopedists employed by a hospital, ziekenhuis, revalidatiecentrum, CLB (Centrum voor Leerlingenbegeleiding) or CPMS (Centre psycho-médico-social) are under a normal employment contract (often public-sector statute for CLB/CPMS, depending on the inrichtende macht). The employer carries the social-security and insurance obligations; the practitioner does not need a KBO number, a btw-nummer, or a sociaal verzekeringsfonds for that activity.

A **RIZIV number is still personal** to the practitioner and is required even for employed logopedists who deliver verstrekkingen in a context where the employer bills them (e.g. ziekenhuis, revalidatiecentrum). It is **not** required for CLB/CPMS work, where the school/CPMS budget — not RIZIV — funds the activity, but most employers still ask for it on hire.

Convention status, in the employed setting, is usually **driven by the employer**: a hospital that adheres to the convention applies the conventietarieven for all its logopedie staff regardless of their personal preference. Self-employed logopedists who do additional employed work in a hospital can therefore have a different effective tariff for the two activities.

A **mixed status** ("hoofdberoep zelfstandig / bijberoep loontrekkende" or vice versa) is common and accepted; the social contributions are calculated on the self-employed income, with adjustments depending on which status is the main one.

## 8. Public registries and programmatic verification

This is the section that matters most for halingo's onboarding flow.

### 8.1 The federal cadastre — eCad

**eCad** is the federal database of healthcare professionals, maintained by the FOD Volksgezondheid / SPF Santé publique. It consolidates identification data, diploma, recognition, visum, social-security data, and (for some professions) specialisations. Per the SPF page on eCad, the back-office is "une application à usage interne" — there is no public REST API for general consumption.

There **is** a public-facing search front-end:

- **Public search engine**: `https://apps.health.belgium.be/ecad-public-search-engine-web/search`
- Local nickname: "the eCad public search" or "Search and register of healthcare providers" (the EN page on `health.belgium.be` links to it).

The public search lets a third party look up a healthcare professional **by name + first name + profession**. It returns whether the person holds a valid visum for that profession. It does **not** publish RIZIV numbers, addresses, or detailed CVs — the public version is a privacy-respecting subset. Sister application **`apps.health.belgium.be/gestautor-public-search/`** ("Gestautor") provides a similar lookup for some professional categories.

### 8.2 The RIZIV "Zoek een zorgverlener" / "Trouver un dispensateur"

RIZIV operates its own public search at `riziv.fgov.be/nl/webtoepassingen/een-zorgverlener-zoeken` (and the parallel FR page). This tool returns, for a given practitioner, their **name, profession, RIZIV number, postal address of practice, and convention status**. It is **the most useful endpoint for halingo** because it ties RIZIV number to identity and to convention status in a single query.

### 8.3 Programmatic verification — what halingo can realistically do

There is **no documented public REST/JSON API** on either eCad or the RIZIV "zoek een zorgverlener" tool as of April 2026. Verification therefore has three practical paths:

1. **Form-driven scraping of the RIZIV public search**, with the user's consent. The tool's HTML is stable enough to parse for a server-side check. This is the approach most Belgian healthtech vendors use.
2. **CSV/Excel bulk extracts from RIZIV** — RIZIV publishes lists of zorgverleners (logopedisten by NIS-code, by convention status) on its open-data pages and on the bevoegdheidscodes pages. Halingo can ingest these on a periodic basis.
3. **eHealth platform integration** — going through the **eHealth-platform** (`ehealth.fgov.be`) gives access to the **CoBRHA** (Common Base Registry for HealthCare Actors), which is the canonical authoritative directory shared by federal and Community partners. CoBRHA access is restricted: the requesting entity must be an "actor" in the eHealth ecosystem, must contract with the eHealth platform, and must justify the basis for processing the personal data. For a SaaS that helps practitioners file their own attesten this is the cleanest long-term answer; for a thin verification step it is overkill.

**Practical halingo recommendation**: at onboarding, ask the user for (a) their name + birthdate, (b) their RIZIV number, then verify by querying the RIZIV public search and matching name + RIZIV. Re-verify every 6 months. For a stronger guarantee, integrate with the eHealth platform once volume justifies the contracting effort.

## 9. Professional bodies (VVL, UPLF, and others)

There is **no federal Belgian umbrella organisation** of logopedists with statutory recognition; the profession is organised on a Community basis.

### 9.1 VVL — Vlaamse Vereniging voor Logopedisten

- **Form**: VZW (non-profit). Founded as the Flemish branch of an older Belgian-wide structure; today the legally recognised representative organisation of logopedisten in Flanders.
- **Site**: `vvl.be`. Secretariat in Belsele (East Flanders).
- **Role**: berroepsbelangenverdediging (representation in negotiations at RIZIV via the Overeenkomstencommissie logopedisten-verzekeringsinstellingen), permanente vorming, ledendiensten, tijdschrift (`Signaal`), modeldocumenten, juridische en deontologische ondersteuning.
- **Membership**: voluntary; large fraction of active Flemish logopedisten are members. Membership unlocks reduced congress and training fees, the doorschrijfkarton for getuigschriften, model contracts, and access to the VVL app.
- **VVL is the Flemish member of ESLA** (see §9.4).
- **Ethische en deontologische commissie** — see §10.

### 9.2 UPLF — Union Professionnelle des Logopèdes Francophones

- **Form**: union professionnelle reconnue (recognised since 1989).
- **Site**: `uplf.be`. Headquartered in Namur.
- **Role**: same dual mission as VVL — interlocutor of the Ministries (FWB, federal Public Health, INAMI), formation continue (the UPLF "Pôle formation" runs an in-person catalogue), juridical support, model documents, journal (`Logopèdes`), accompaniement of students and young professionals.
- **Members**: > 1,700 (figure published by UPLF). Membership is voluntary.
- **UPLF is the Francophone member of ESLA**.

### 9.3 Other associations

- **DSL / German-speaking Community**: there is **no large German-language professional union of logopedists in Belgium**. German-speaking logopèdes typically affiliate with UPLF (because they trained in the FWB) or with the German DBL (Deutscher Bundesverband für Logopädie) for cross-border content.
- **Federal umbrella**: a "Belgische Federatie van Logopedisten" / "Fédération Belge des Logopèdes" is sometimes mentioned in older documents, but there is no current active federal-level NGO with operational scope. VVL and UPLF coordinate informally (e.g. when negotiating the national convention) and via ESLA.
- **Specialty groups**: a number of subject-matter VZW's exist (e.g. on stuttering, on developmental language disorders, on swallowing) but they are scientific societies, not licensing bodies.

### 9.4 International — ESLA (formerly CPLOL)

The historical European federation **CPLOL** (Comité Permanent de Liaison des Orthophonistes-Logopèdes de l'Union Européenne, founded 6 March 1988) was **dissolved on 6 March 2021** and immediately succeeded by **ESLA — European Speech and Language Therapy Association** (`eslaeurope.eu`). ESLA currently lists **37 member associations from 34 countries representing > 40,000 SLT professionals**. **Both VVL and UPLF are listed as Belgian member associations.**

### 9.5 Mandatory? — short answer

**Membership in VVL or UPLF is not legally mandatory.** A logopedist with a visum and RIZIV number can practice and bill without being a member. However, in practice the unions are *the* channel for the practitioner's voice in the convention negotiations, and many employers (and most quality-label projects) expect membership. Halingo should not block onboarding on union membership but can usefully ask about it for analytics.

## 10. Code of ethics and disciplinary framework

### 10.1 Is there a formal Order? — No.

Unlike physicians (Orde der Artsen / Ordre des Médecins, statutory under the Loi du 25 juillet 1938 modified) or pharmacists (Orde der Apothekers / Ordre des Pharmaciens), **logopedie does not have a statutory Order in Belgium**. There is no Belgian "Orde der Logopedisten" with disciplinary powers, no equivalent of the Provinciale Raden, no equivalent of the Raad van Beroep. Discussions to create one have surfaced periodically — most recently in the 2021 Advice 2021-01 of the Federale Raad voor Paramedische Beroepen — but no legislative initiative has materialised.

The consequence is that **disciplinary action against a logopedist for ethical breaches is not handled by a profession-specific Order**. Avenues are:

- the **internal Ethische en Deontologische Commissie** of VVL or UPLF (members only; sanctions limited to expulsion from the union — no impact on the visum);
- **withdrawal of the visum** by FOD Volksgezondheid via the federal Toezichtcommissie under the coordinated law of 10 May 2015 (reserved for serious cases);
- **civil and criminal liability** before ordinary courts for malpractice, fraud, breach of professional secrecy (Article 458 Strafwetboek / Code pénal — applies to logopedists like any healthcare professional);
- **suspension or removal of the RIZIV registration** by the **Dienst voor geneeskundige evaluatie en controle (DGEC) van het RIZIV** for billing fraud or non-compliance with the nomenclature.

### 10.2 The code of ethics

A **"Ethische en deontologische code van de logopedisten"** exists. It was originally drafted by the Belgische Vereniging voor Logopedie / Association Belge de Logopédie in the early 2000s, and is today maintained by **VVL's Ethische en Deontologische Commissie (EDC)** (`vvl.be/organisatie/ethische-en-deontologische-commissie`, contact `edc@vvl.be`). It is published as a PDF on VVL's site and is mirrored in member-facing intranets and on third-party sites such as `de-piramide.be`. The current PDF metadata indicate creation in September 2006 with substantive updates around 2012.

**Legal status**: the code is **binding on VVL members** as a contractual obligation flowing from membership, and is **persuasive** for non-members (judges and the DGEC routinely cite it as the standaard van goed vakmanschap). It is **not** binding by force of law in the way the Code de déontologie médicale becomes binding when codified by Royal Decree.

Key obligations under the code (high-level): respect for human dignity and patient autonomy, professional secrecy, evidence-based practice, honest billing, continuous training, no fee-splitting, refusal of conflicts of interest, transparent advertising, collegiality with other professions.

UPLF maintains a parallel **Code de déontologie** for its francophone members with substantively similar content.

### 10.3 What halingo needs to model

For compliance features, halingo can treat the VVL/UPLF code as the de facto deontological reference and link out to the official PDFs from any in-app help text on ethics. Disciplinary status is **not publicly queryable** — the eCad does not expose ethics decisions.

## 11. Continuing education

### 11.1 The legal hook

Article 3 of KB 20.10.1994 explicitly mentions that the recognised logopedist must "maintain and update professional knowledge and skills" through continuing education. The federal coordinated law of 10 May 2015 also makes "permanente vorming" a general obligation for all healthcare professionals. **There is, however, no federally-decreed minimum number of hours, no point system imposed by RIZIV, and no agréation of courses by the Communities** for logopedie (in stark contrast to general practice physicians, who have an INAMI accreditation system with mandatory CME).

### 11.2 In practice — what does CPD look like?

CPD for logopedists is **professionally expected and self-regulated**:

- VVL and UPLF run extensive in-house **vormings-/formation catalogs** (multi-day congresses, study days, in-house workshops, e-learnings) and award internal participation certificates. VVL specifies CPD as one of its four core missions.
- The **Ethische en deontologische code** treats lifelong learning as a deontological duty.
- Most **employers** (hospitals, revalidatiecentra, CLB) impose internal CPD targets in the staff regulations.
- A small but growing number of **RIZIV-side incentives** are linked to CPD: the Overeenkomstencommissie has discussed in recent years coupling part of the sociaal statuut to attended CPD, but as of the 2024–2025 convention this is **not yet implemented** for logopedie. Halingo should not assume a hard CPD-points-to-RIZIV link today; the connection is reputational and contractual, not statutory.

### 11.3 Course accreditation

There is **no central accrediting body** for logopedie courses in Belgium. VVL/UPLF assess and badge their own offerings; universities and hogescholen offer postgraduates and specialised training (e.g. KU Leuven postgraduaat slikstoornissen, ULg/UCL post-graduate in neurolinguistics) that members can use. **Halingo CPD-tracking features should therefore be flexible**: they cannot rely on a national CPD ID, and they should accept attestations from VVL, UPLF, ESLA, universities, and recognised foreign bodies.

### 11.4 Comparison with the medical profession

For context: a Belgian general practitioner accumulates **20 accreditation points/year** through the RIZIV accreditation scheme to maintain eligibility for accreditation honoraria. A logopedist has **no analogous national mechanism**. This is something the Federal Council for Paramedical Professions has flagged as a gap (advice 2021-01).

## 12. Professional liability insurance

### 12.1 Is it legally mandatory?

For logopedists in Belgium, **professional civil liability insurance (BA Beroep / RC professionnelle) is not imposed by federal statute**. Compare this with physicians, where the Wet Patiëntenrechten (22 augustus 2002) and the Wet medische ongevallen (31 maart 2010) create strong de facto pressure but technically require coverage only in specific contexts; logopedists are even less constrained.

It is, however:

- **strongly recommended**, and treated as a deontological duty under the VVL/UPLF codes;
- **practically required** by every hospital, revalidatiecentrum, CLB and group practice that an employed or contractor logopedist might join;
- **expected by judges and patients** in any malpractice case — uninsured practice is itself a sign of negligence in Belgian case law.

### 12.2 What the market offers

The benchmark policy, used for many years by both VVL and UPLF in framework agreements, is **Curalia × Baloise "BA Beroep Logopedisten en Audiologen"**, with **DAS** as the legal-assistance underwriter. Typical 2026 conditions:

- **Premium**: ~€100/year for an active logopedist (self-employed or employed), all costs included;
- **Civil liability — bodily injury**: €5,000,000 per claim / €10,000,000 per year;
- **Civil liability — material damage**: €250,000 per claim / €500,000 per year;
- **Legal assistance**: €25,000 per claim for criminal defence / civil defence / civil recovery / property; €15,000 per claim for insurance / disciplinary / RIZIV disputes.

Other providers (Belfius, BNP Paribas Fortis, AG Insurance, DVV) offer comparable products. Group rates are typically negotiated by VVL/UPLF for members.

### 12.3 Other insurances commonly bundled

- **Gewaarborgd inkomen / revenu garanti**: covers loss of income on incapacity. Highly recommended for self-employed practitioners (the basic INSZ-arbeidsongeschiktheid coverage for self-employed is modest).
- **Materiële schade / property insurance** for the cabinet.
- **Cyber/data-protection liability** — increasingly relevant given that logopedists process medical data and use SaaS like halingo. Curalia and BNP Paribas Fortis sell add-ons; the GBA/APD has been more active since 2023 in fining healthcare professionals who fail to secure patient data.

### 12.4 Recommendation for halingo

When halingo onboards a self-employed logopedist, it should (a) request confirmation that BA Beroep is in place, (b) capture the insurer name and policy number for record, (c) provide a clear notice that halingo as a SaaS does not extend coverage to user errors. This is also good evidence in the GDPR controller/processor relationship.
