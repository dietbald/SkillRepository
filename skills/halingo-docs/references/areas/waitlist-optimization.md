# Waitlist Optimization

## What this area covers

The lightweight waitlist surface — a status flag on a dossier, a list filter that surfaces waitlisted patients, and a count for at-a-glance situational awareness.

## Features in this area

- Status flag — mark a patient dossier as "on waitlist" so the therapist remembers the patient is awaiting an intake slot.
- List filter — filter the patient roster to show only waitlisted patients.
- Statistics count — show how many patients are currently on the waitlist.

## Key product behaviors

- Waitlist is **a flag on the dossier**, not a separate intake queue. There is no priority order, no scheduling automation, no SMS / email outreach to waitlisted patients.
- A waitlisted patient still has a full dossier; the flag simply hides them from the active-caseload view until intake.
- The therapist clears the flag when the patient is brought into active treatment.

## Cross-references

- Patient Data Privacy — waitlisted patients are dossiers under the standard access-control model.
- Practice Analytics — the waitlist count is one of the per-practice patient stats surfaces.
- Treatment Planning — the patient typically moves off the waitlist when the first treatment is created.
