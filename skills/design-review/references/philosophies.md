# 20 Design Philosophies

Five schools, four philosophies each. Each entry: **Name** · *Core principle* · Rules.

When reviewing, first identify which philosophy the design is targeting (or should be), then judge fidelity to *that one* — not against all twenty.

---

## School 1 — Information Architecture (信息建筑派)

### 01. Pentagram — Michael Bierut Style
- **Core**: Extreme typographic hierarchy on Swiss grids.
- **Rules**: 60%+ whitespace · black/white + one accent colour · typography drives the page (no decorative imagery substituting for type).

### 02. Stamen Design
- **Core**: Cartographic logic applied to data visualisation; organic patterns over geometric.
- **Rules**: Warm palette (terracotta, sage, deep blue) · layered information systems · maps and data are first-class, not illustrations.

### 03. Information Architects (iA)
- **Core**: Content-first; every decoration is suspect.
- **Rules**: System fonts only · classic blue hyperlinks · reading column ~66 characters · zero ornamentation.

### 04. Fathom Information Design
- **Core**: Every pixel must carry information. Scientific precision.
- **Rules**: Neutral palette · precise data viz with annotated axes · citations integrated into the design, not appended.

---

## School 2 — Motion Poetics (运动诗学派)

### 05. Locomotive
- **Core**: Scrolling as narrative journey; parallax depth for storytelling, not decoration.
- **Rules**: Generous vertical spacing · dark backgrounds · glowing accents · 100vh hero sections that earn their height.

### 06. Active Theory
- **Core**: 3D particle systems representing real-time data flow.
- **Rules**: WebGL · neon gradients (cyan/magenta) · mouse-reactive elements · depth-of-field effects.

### 07. Field.io
- **Core**: Generative art systems producing algorithmically unique compositions.
- **Rules**: Monochromatic base + one vibrant accent · mathematical precision · Voronoi / parametric patterns.

### 08. Resn
- **Core**: Gamified user journeys with illustrative storytelling.
- **Rules**: Warm palette · character-driven · scroll-triggered animations that progress a narrative.

---

## School 3 — Minimalism (极简主义派)

### 09. Experimental Jetset
- **Core**: One idea = one form. Primary-colour constraint.
- **Rules**: Red / blue / yellow + black / white (Mondrian palette) · no photography · grid-based · type as content.

### 10. Müller-Brockmann Swiss Modernism
- **Core**: Mathematical objectivity through grid systems. 8pt baseline.
- **Rules**: Strict alignment · two-colour maximum · no decorative elements · Akzidenz-Grotesk or close equivalent.

### 11. Build (Michael C. Place)
- **Core**: Refined simplicity; luxury-level craft.
- **Rules**: 70%+ whitespace · subtle weight shifts (200–600) · golden-ratio proportions · obsessive detail.

### 12. Sagmeister & Walsh
- **Core**: Joy through unexpected colour bursts on a minimal base.
- **Rules**: Handmade + digital fusion · optimistic copy · experimental yet readable typography · one big surprise per page.

---

## School 4 — Experimental Avant-Garde (实验先锋派)

### 13. Zach Lieberman
- **Core**: Programming as painting; hand-drawn algorithmic aesthetics.
- **Rules**: Black/white only · real-time generation · visible grid/process · poetic algorithms.

### 14. Raven Kwok
- **Core**: System beauty surpasses individual beauty. Parametric design.
- **Rules**: Fractal / recursive structures · high-contrast black/white · architectural data viz.

### 15. Ash Thorp
- **Core**: Future as solitary poetry. Cinematic concept art.
- **Rules**: Film-grade lighting · warm cyberpunk (orange/teal) · industrial luxury · volumetric effects.

### 16. Territory Studio
- **Core**: Imagining tomorrow's interfaces. Fantasy UI (FUI).
- **Rules**: Holographic aesthetics · orange/amber or cyan accents · overlapping data layers · futures that read as believable, not Hollywood.

---

## School 5 — Eastern Philosophy (东方哲学派)

### 17. Takram
- **Core**: Technology as medium for philosophical inquiry.
- **Rules**: Elegant prototypes · soft tech (rounded corners) · charts-as-art · natural neutral palette.

### 18. Kenya Hara
- **Core**: Design is clearing, not filling. Emptiness as design.
- **Rules**: 80%+ whitespace · paper textures · layered whites (warm / cool / off-white) · Zen subtraction.

### 19. Irma Boom
- **Core**: Non-linear information as physical poetry; challenge the grid.
- **Rules**: Boundary games · unexpected colour combos (pink + red) · handcraft translated to screen · dense exploration over reduction.

### 20. Neo Shen
- **Core**: Technology requiring human warmth via Eastern aesthetics.
- **Rules**: Digital ink-wash effects · soft glows · poetic negative space · emotional palette (deep blue / warm grey / soft gold).

---

## How to pick a philosophy when reviewing

1. Look at the *intent signals* — is the design typography-led? Motion-led? Data-led? Spatial?
2. Match to the school first, then narrow within the school.
3. State your pick in one sentence with the signal that drove it ("Type-led, mostly black/white with one red accent, 65% whitespace → Pentagram").
4. If the design is mid-way between two philosophies, name both and review against the dominant one. Hybrids that don't commit usually score low on Originality and Philosophy Alignment.
